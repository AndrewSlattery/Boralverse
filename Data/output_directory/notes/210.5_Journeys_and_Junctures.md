210.5: Journeys and Junctures

notes on various aspects of the Boralverse alternate history setting which begin with the letter J.

Jaibos Breweries (Borlish: Yeustman Jaibos) is a beer-making guild based in the southwestern Borlish city of Axbane operating under the ownership of the Jacobin Fraternity. The Fraternity was founded in tenth-century Flanders under the patronage of Amelia of Bruges, but was forced to flee to Borland in 1067 N after being implicated in a popular uprising against Drengot Emperor Geoffrey II; it thereafter established its principal seat at the Peninsular Monastery on a promontory overlooking Axbane.

Japetos III is an barric steeplepost line [telegraph cable] running undersea between the port of Gadire [Agadir] in Morrack and the city of Paratzon [Saint John] in Brasil [Newfoundland], a distance of some 1,500 leagues. It is the third cable to bear the name Iapetos, a Titan who as the father of Atlas has supplied a ready name for successive endeavours to span the Atlantic by wire. The project was jointly conceived in 1943 N by the Morrack and Ambrosian governments, but was set back first by protracted disagreements over funding between the two administrations and then by the outbreak of war, before the cable at last conveyed its first messages in October 1950 N.

Josforo Sant'Angelo was a traveller and writer from Tuscany, and the youngest son of the last Marksman of Cremuna—a title that lapsed when the Lombard marquisates were abolished in the 1860s. Sant'Angelo recorded one of his travels in Pasti Mezziterreni (Middlesea Meals, 1880 N), a mealbook documenting a single sunward journey along the full arc of the Middlesea [Mediterranean] coast, from Tarifa in Andalus to the Atlantic-facing port of X̀r l Joas [Ksar es-Seghir] in Morrack.

Judeta Barracin  was a novelist born in Andalus who spent her formative years among the islands of the Arcabil [the Caribbean], the daughter of a trading family with commercial interests in those ports. She returned to Andalus as an adult with a sharp eye for the social textures of its cities. Her 1919 N trevold [novel] Querdos Llineros (Flaxen Hearts) made was an immediate sensation: set in early-nineteenth-century Cordova [Córdoba] and following a cast of market traders, minor gentry, and household staff through a web of interconnected small dramas, it is now considered a founding text of social comedy alongside the contemporaneous output of Dorotha Quistack.
