231.5: The Riverine Cities

excerpt from Downstream: Afterlives of the Riverine Cities, a 2019 retrospective history by Zorica Branimirova, professor in the department of Domain History at the University of Belgrade.

…two centuries of unified rule under the Kingdom of the Danaw, which reached a zenith with the Decadence of Belgrade as it incorporated Bulgary [Bulgaria] and pushed back its border with Bavarn [Bavaria]. This kingdom did not survive the Second German War (1815–23). What rose in its place a federacy of city-states along the river bound by treaty, trade, and a rather pointed distrust of distant rulers claiming authority over the great expanse of the basin.

Hosting significant religious minorities long resented by, and resentful of, local baronial powers, the Cities became a hotbed of dissent. Promethean clubs, precursors to what would become the Deviance movement, had taken root even in the erstwhile kingdom; their successors flourished openly enough that by the 1810s the Cities hosted a genuinely semi-public tovaric [queer] subculture. The word tov itself is first attested in this milieu.

Belgrade had become an obvious host for the international committee that produced the Laic Declarations of 1850, negotiating the unyoking of religious and administrative authority. Forty years later the Cities were, alongside Britain and New Leudong, among the earliest adopters of the Household Renovation (the broader loosening of marriage and family norms). In 1912 Boxa [Novi Sad], often cited as the most cosmopolitan of the Cities, opened the earliest house of odyssey [cinema] to sell tickets to the public on a regular schedule.

What became of the Cities themselves? Upriver, Pojon [Bratislava] and Strengom [Esztergom] had by 1920 been absorbed into Moravy [Moravia] and Awry [Hungary] respectively by means of the Millstone War's bloody reorganisation. Both cities nonetheless kept, and keep, independent courts and powers over taxation comparable to the Borlish cities of Axbane and Pentrose.

Meanwhile, the Sava Compact of 1932 created the Danaw Concert further downriver, which retains a greater part of the character of the Riverine Cities. Intransigent Boxa declined full membership; its 1935 association protocol is the bane of cartographers the world over, who would prefer Boxa be either a fully sovereign city-state or fully subordinate…
