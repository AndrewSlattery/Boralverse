215.5: Ministry for Consequences

excerpt from The Structure of Twentieth-Century Polities, a 2024 reference work by Neuwillem historian Mbaiara Hormelingh, translated from the original Willem (Stellingen van Twintixtéw Státsvorm) into Merch by Jet Torpenhow.

…first Ministry for Consequences to be established was that of the Drengot Collusion, the alliance-federacy of northwestern Europe whose dramatic collapse in 1961 is covered in more detail in the section of Ministries for Information in chapter 4. The last of the twenty-four ministries of the Collusion to be created, it was set up in 1932 under the leadership of Runcibal Sawyer-Boon (nowadays eclipsed in fame by his grand-daughter Vendetta Boon). 

The original remit of the Ministry for Consequences was purely reactive: it was to monitor the impact of policies implemented by laws and by the decrees of other ministries (e.g. Family, Farming or Wealth) and to publish reports on their effects. Institutions with similar functions had existed in many governments for centuries (and arguably millennia, in the cases of Cathay, China and the Persias), though they were rarely so centralised and so independent in their activities. Despite its later failings, the Collusion's anthracite government in the 1930s recognised that Consequences would have to maintain editorial autonomy or else be subject to censorship from the very government it was intended to monitor.

At the time of writing, Ministries for Consequences have been established in several dozen polities worldwide, from the Ministry for Effectiveness in Tavancy [~Incan Empire] to the Ministry for the Future in Nackon Dai [Thailand].
