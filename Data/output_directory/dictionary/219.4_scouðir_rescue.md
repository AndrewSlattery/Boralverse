219.4: scouðir "rescue"

scouðir /xuˈðɪr/ [xʊˈðɪː]
- rescue, save, deliver, to remove from a dangerous situation;
- buy back, redeem, to convert some security back into (e.g. cash, a prize or a pawned item);
- bail out, pull out, to withdraw someone from a commitment before it worsens;
- snap out of, jolt out of, to abruptly end a confused or mistaken psychological state

Etymology: via Old Borlish directly from Latin excutio "I drive out, throw off" (via the notion of causing someone to leave their current circumstances). In modern use the word usually implies that the rescuee either got themselves into trouble or that they were unfairly tricked into a bad situation.
