cubicr /ki틛b톩kr퍌/ [k톩틛b톩.k톓]
- bedsit, studio, a single rented room serving as an entire dwelling;
- cell, a small private room in a monastery or similar institution;
- berth, cabin, compartment, a small room sectioned off aboard a ship or train;
- snug, bolt-hole, a small space kept as one's own private refuge

Etymology: Middle Borlish, originally in the monastic sense, borrowed from Latin cubiculum "bedroom". This is a derivation from cub콄 "I lie down, sleep". Extension to naval use (first for e.g. captain's cabins, later for more lowly accommodation) is attested from the sixteenth century, with further expansion to other transport and to simple housing in the nineteenth century.
