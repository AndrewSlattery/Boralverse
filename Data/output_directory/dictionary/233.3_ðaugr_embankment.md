233.3: ðaugr "embankment"

ðaugr /ˈðogr̩/ [ˈðo.gɐ]
- (historical) rampart, earthwork, a defensive bank raised about a fort or camp;
- levee, dyke, embankment, a raised bank confining a river or holding back the sea;
- causeway, viaduct, a raised bank carrying a road or railway across low ground;
- (sport) terrace, end, a banked standing area for spectators at a ground

Etymology: from Old Borlish ðargre "rampart", originally only in military use. This comes from a Vulgar Latin adargerem, from a variant arger of Classical agger "rubble, defensive wall". The term was first extended to coastal structures in the dialects of the southern towns in the Middle Borlish period. Usage in sports is seen from the nineteenth century.
