185.4: bastegl "bulwark"

bastegl /baňąst…™jl/ [baňąst…™jl]
- fortress, fortification, a strongly built and defended military structure or enclosure used in warfare;
- bastion, stronghold, a secure position or place of power and influence;
- bulwark, defence, something that serves as a shield or protection against danger or attack;
- safeguard, protection, precaution, any means of preventing harm or ensuring safety

Etymology: a borrowing from Middle French bastille "fortress", itself from early Doccian [Occitan] bastida "fortress, edifice" adapted to the -ille suffix < Latin ńęle "enclosure". This comes from Late Latin bastiŇć "I build; I sew", a borrowing from Frankish. In metaphorical usage the word is in evidence from the eighteenth century.
