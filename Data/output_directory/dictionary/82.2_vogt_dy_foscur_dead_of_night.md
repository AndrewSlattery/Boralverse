82.2: vogt dy foscur "dead of night"

vogt dy foscur /v…Ējt di foňąx…™r/ [v…Ējt d…™ f äňąx…™ňź]
- pitch blackness, inky blackness, a particularly deep darkness;
- dead of night, small hours, witching hour, the part of the night conceived of as the darkest and farthest from day

Etymology: idiom first attested in the poetry of 15C writer Drominir, literally "emptiness of darkness". The noun vogt descends from Vulgar Latin *vocitus "empty", a variant of the synonymous vacuus in Classical Latin. The noun foscur is a noun derivative of adjective fosc "dark" which descends from Latin fuscus "dimly lit, black".
