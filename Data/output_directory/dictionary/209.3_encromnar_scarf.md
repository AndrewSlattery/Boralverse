209.3: encromnar "scarf"

encromnar /ňĆ…õn.kr…Ēmňąnar/ [ňĆ…õŇč.k Ä ämňąn…źňź]
- pocket, to put into a pouch or pocket;
- stow, put away, lodge, to place something in a space-saving manner;
- (slang) wolf, scoff, scarf, to eat (especially an impressive amount or with impressive speed)

Etymology: Middle Borlish, verbal derivation from cromn "pocket, purse, small bag". This descends uncomplicatedly from Latin crumena "pouch for carrying money". Metaphorical extension of this word is seen from the fifteenth century; in the slang sense it most recently became popular in recent decades.
