94.2: moglastr "moist"

moglastr /m…Ējňąla.strŐ©/ [m äjňąla.st…ź]
- damp, moist, somewhat wet (usually to an undesired extent);
- (of the weather) damp, wet, miserable, as of the period between showers or shortly after rainfall;
- weepy, tearful, lachrymose, inclined to crying and sadness

Etymology: derivative of moglar "to wet, dampen, moisten" with the pejorative suffix -astr < Latin -aster "resembling, quasi-". This verb descends from Vulgar Latin *molliŇć "I soak, drench", related to mollis "soft, tender".
