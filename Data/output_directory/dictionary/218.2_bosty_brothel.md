218.2: bosty "brothel"

bosty /bɔˈsti/ [bʊˈsti]
- brothel, bordello, an establishment where people engage in sexual activity for pay;
- mess, shambles, jumble, disorder, a collections of things in a confused or dirty state;
- (interjection) what the hell, an expression of shock or incomprehension at how bad or undesirable some situation is

Etymology: from Middle Borlish bosty "brothel", further origin disputed. Possibly from an unattested northern dialect *bosteul, *bostib, which would reflect Latin prostibulum "prostitute, brothel". Alternatively from an unattested southern dialect *bosty borrowed from a contemporary Welsh compound of bos "lump, knob; (poss.) penis?" and ty "house", the former element itself coming via Old French boce "lump". Metaphorical extension to situations considered similar to a notional brothel is attested from the seventeenth century.
