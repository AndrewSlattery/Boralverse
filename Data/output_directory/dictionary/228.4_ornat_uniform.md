228.4: ornat "uniform"

ornat /ɔrˈnat/ [ʊːˈnat]
- livery, uniform, a distinctive outfit which serves to identify members of a particular group of people;
- regalia, finery, magnificent or expensive garments or accessories;
- fancy dress, apparel, costume, getup, a distinctive set of clothes worn for a particular purpose (especially entertainment)

Etymology: Middle Borlish, borrowed from Latin ōrnātus "decoration, furniture", a noun derivation of ōrnō "I equip, decorate". Originally specialised to insignia and other superficial identifying features, the word has generalised to uniforms as a whole by the seventeenth century.
