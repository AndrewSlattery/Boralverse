231.2: cochiglot "snail"

cochiglot /ˌko.kaiˈlɔt/ [ˌko.kɐiˈlɔt]
- snail, any of many gastropods with a coiled shell;
- sluggard, slowpoke, slowcoach, a person who takes a long time to do or realise things;
- ampersand, a ligature of <et> representing the word "and";
- codpiece, cup, box, a rigid protective garment covering the penis and testicles

Etymology: Middle Borlish, diminutive of cochigl "shell; spiral", from Late Latin cocchilia "shell", a combination of Classical conchȳlium "shellfish" and coccum "scarlet berry, insect". In the first sense it displaced part of the sematic space of limaç "slug, (obsolete or dialect0 snail", which reflects synonymous līmāx in Latin. Metaphorical use for slow people is seen from the seventeenth century; the latter senses are presumed to be independent extensions of the notion of a "small spiral" and "small shell", respectively.
