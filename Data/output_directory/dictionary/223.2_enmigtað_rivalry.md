223.2: enmigtað "rivalry"

enmigtað /ˌɛn.maiˈtaθ/ [ˌɛn.mɐiˈtaθ]
- enmity, hostility, the condition of being enemies or at cross-purposes;
- rivalry, competitiveness, a desire to defeat one's opponents;
- rancour, animosity, a feeling of ire for someone over a perceived wrongdoing

Etymology: Middle Borlish, noun derivation from enmig "enemy, rival". This descends directly from Latin inimīcus "enemy", literally "un-friend". For the sense developments, compare parallel constructions in other Romance languages such as Portingale nemizade or Norman enmisté "rivalry".
