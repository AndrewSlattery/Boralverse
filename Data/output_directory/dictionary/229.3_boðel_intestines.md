229.3: boðel "intestines"

boðel /boˈðɛl/ [bʊˈðɛl]
- intestines, guts, bowels, the lower digestive tract of a person or animal;
- piping, pipe, hose, conduit, a flexible tube for conveying e.g. fluids or wires;
- labyrinth, warren, maze, a complex network of passages;
- (culinary) the casing of a sausage; (by extension) a receptacle stuffed to bursting or an overcrowded room

Etymology: via Old Borlish boðel "intestines" from Vulgar Latin botellus "small sausage", diminutive of Classical botulus "sausage, black pudding". Metaphorical extension to other items resembling the bowels either in form or arrangement is seen from as early as the thirteenth century.
