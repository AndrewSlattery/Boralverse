225.4: semblant "ilk"

semblant /s…õmňąblant/ [s…™mňąblan(t)]
- ilk, kind, sort, type, a category to which something belongs;
- appearance, look, aspect, the visible form something or someone presents to others;
- mien, countenance, expression, the look on a person's face or (by extension) their presumed emotional state

Etymology: from Old French semblant "appearance; resemblance; the look on one's face", originally the present participle of sembler "to seem, resemble", itself from synonymous similńĀre in Vulgar Latin, a refashioning of Classical similis "like, similar". In the modern language the term usually has dismissive or otherwise deprecating connotations.
