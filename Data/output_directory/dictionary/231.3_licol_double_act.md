231.3: licol "double act"

licol /liˈkɔl/ [lɪˈkɔl]
- halter, lead, a collar of straps or rope placed on the head of animals to lead or tie them;
- brace, yoke, a set of two animals as might be tied with halters or carried together (as of game birds);
- pair, couple, twosome, duo, double act, a set of two people or things in particular contexts (as of wheels, performers, jewellery or architecture);
- a score of two points in various games or sports, or an action which is awarded such a score

Etymology: from Middle Borlish licol "yoke, halter tying two animals together", borrowed from synonymous liecol in contemporary French. This is an exocentric compound of lier "tie, join, link" ← Latin ligō "I bind" and col "neck" ← Latin collum "neck, throat". Extension to other collections of two things proceeded via hunting to sports and games and finally to performing arts.
