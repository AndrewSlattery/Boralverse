221.3: teisment "braid"

teisment /tiňązm…õnt/ [t…™ňązm…õnt]
- braid, plait, interwoven strands of e.g. twine or hair;
- wreath, an ornamental circular band made of plaited strands;
- device, scheme, stratagem, a plan to gain the upper hand in a situation;
- deduction, inference, a sequence of logical steps from a set of premises to a conclusion;
- narrative, story, plot, a recounted sequence of events;
- project, undertaking, an endeavour with a specific goal

Etymology: Old Borlish, a nominalisation in all senses of the verb teissir "weave; concoct; infer; narrate". This descends with a change in conjugation (compare contemporaneous tisre "weave") from Latin texŇć "I knit, I intertwine".
