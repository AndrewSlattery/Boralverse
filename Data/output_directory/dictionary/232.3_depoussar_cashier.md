232.3: depoussar "cashier"

depoussar /ňĆde.puňąsar/ [ňĆde.p äňąs…źňź]
- drive off, beat back, force a person or animal to leave or to remain at a distance;
- wean, take a young animal off the teat;
- cashier, dismiss, fire, remove someone from a post or office;
- rebuff, turn away, refuse a request or suitor outright

Etymology: borrowed in all senses from Latin dńďpulsus, past participle of dńďpellŇć "I drive out, expel; I deter; I wean", then adapted to existing and related appoussar "push, drive" and repoussar "push back, repel". Nowadays the terms is seen principally in military or romantic contexts, or occasionally in agriculture (where it competes with sevrar "cut off, separate").
