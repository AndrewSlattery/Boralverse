222.3: engin "nature"

engin /ɛnˈʒɪn/ [ɪɲˈʝɪn]
- nature, character, an innate quality;
- disposition, inclination, habit, the way in which someone is apt to behave;
- talent, ability, skill, unpractised capability;
- (historical) onager, catapult, battering ram, any of several types of siege engine

Etymology: from Old Borlish engeȝn "talent; siege machine", inherited in the first sense from Latin ingenium "nature, cleverness" and borrowed from Old French cognate engin "cleverness; siege machine" (from the same Latin word) in the second sense. The historical use is based on first-millennium writers such as the younger Isidore, who copied it from late Classical authors. The sense "nature" was reinforced by theological writing in the Second Tetrarchy period (1028–c.1200) which contrasted ingenium "nature" with accidentia "happenstance".
