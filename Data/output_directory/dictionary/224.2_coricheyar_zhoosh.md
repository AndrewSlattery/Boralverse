224.2: coricheyar "zhoosh"

coricheyar /ňĆko.riňąkjar/ [ňĆko. Ä…™ňąkj…źňź]
- (of fabric) to decorate with chintz patterns;
- (of a person) to dress in chintz-patterned fabric;
- (of a room) to fill with chintz-patterned items of furniture;
- (colloquial) zhoosh, spruce, finesse, spice up, to make more appealing or exciting

Etymology: 19C, verbal derivation from noun coricot "chintz (fabric, clothing, furniture)", as though the -ot ending were a diminutive suffix. The noun comes from the city of Coricot [Kozhikode] in Limerick [~Kerala, India], from where the fabric was exported. In metaphorical usage the term is seen from the 1950s.
