144.2: cavir yert "boot up"

cavir yert /kaňąv…™r j…õrt/ [k…źňąv…™ňź  Ě…õňźt]
- wake up, come to, stir, to regain consciousness;
- turn on, switch on, activate, start, boot up, to begin operating;
- (of a business) open, to start serving customers

Etymology: early Modern Borlish idiom literally meaning "to get awake". The verb cavir "get" is a direct descendant of capiŇć "take, pick, recieve", used in the sense "become" since the Middle Borlish period. The adjective yert "awake, alert; (of a business) open" descends via Old Borlish yerte "standing, upright, vertical" and synonymous ńďrctus in Vulgar Latin from Classical ńďrńďctus "raised, built, erected". Extended from people to machinery and thence to businesses by the nineteenth century.
