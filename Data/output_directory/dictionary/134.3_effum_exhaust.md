effum /…õňąf…™m/ […™ňąf…™m]
- exhaust, the gas released from a combustion engine;
- outgas, a gas emitted by any means (especially when considered as an unwanted waste product);
- hot air, bluster, bombast, long-winded or officious talk having no real substance

Etymology: borrowing in the 18th century from Scholastic Latin noun effŇęmus "emitted smoke, steam" and verb effŇęmŇć "I emit smoke, steam". These are derived from fŇęmus "smoke, steam" with the prefix ex- "out, away from". In metaphorical use the word is attested from the late 19th century.
