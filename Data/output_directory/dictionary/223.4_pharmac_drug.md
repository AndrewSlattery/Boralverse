223.4: pharmac "drug"

pharmac /farˈmak/ [fɐːˈmak]
- medication, pharmaceutical, drug, medicine, a substance ingested or injected to heal or otherwise affect the health of a patient;
- (historical) charm, talisman, fetish, amulet, an item which has been enchanted to have a magical effect (espeically promoting harm or healing);
- remedy, fix, solution, silver bullet, an unexpected method of solving a seemingly intractable problem

Etymology: Medieval Borlish, borrowed from Latin pharmacum "drug; poison", from Greek φάρμακον • phármakon "drug; potion; dye". The historical use persists in parachthon [e.g. fantasy] writing, having fallen into disuse in post-Arnoutszen medicine; the metaphorical extension is attested from as early as the sixteenth century.
