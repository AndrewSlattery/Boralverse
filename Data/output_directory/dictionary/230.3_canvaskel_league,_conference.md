230.3: canvaskel "league, conference"

canvaskel /ňĆkan.vasňąk…õl/ [ňĆka…Ī.v…źsňąk…õl]
- league, conference, an association of sports teams which compete against each other;
- ranking, scoreboard, leaderboard, a list of competitors ordered by how well they have performed;
- hierarchy, totem pole, a system of organisation with superiors and inferiors;
- (sociology) the way society as a whole engages with a given concept, especially how systems use it to assign status or enforce specific roles 

Etymology: nineteenth century in a sports context (originally lineball), a borrowing from Welsh canvaskel, a compound of canva "stile, rung, footstool" and skel "ladder". The former is a derivation of cam "step, footstep", while the latter is an early Middle Welsh borrowing from Norman French eskele "ladder". Metaphorical extension is seen from the early twentieth century, while the final sense came out of the Mehannin [Brisbane] academic school in the 1970s.
