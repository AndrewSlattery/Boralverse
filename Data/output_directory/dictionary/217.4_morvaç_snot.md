217.4: morvaç "snot"

morvaç /mɔrˈvats/ [mʊːˈvats]
- snot, mucus, bogey, boogers, a secretion from the nose;
- glanders, strangles, any of several infectious diseases of e.g. horses presenting with nasal discharge;
- brat, rascal, imp, urchin, scamp, a child regarded as unruly or selfish

Etymology: pejorative derivation from earlier morf "snot". Possibly a direct descendant of Latin morbus "disease", but also connected to Old French morve "snot, glanders", which may instead be a metathetic borrowing from Old Doccian vorm (compare Barcelon vorm "glanders, jellyfish"), from Frankish wurm "worm". The word has been used for misbehaving children since the 16th century.
