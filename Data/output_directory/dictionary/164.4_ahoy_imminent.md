164.4: ahoy "imminent"

ahoy /aňąh…Ēi/ […źňąh…Ēi]
- ahoy, (nautical) interjection to warn of something coming into sight or approaching;
- in view, visible, within sight as from a lookout;
- nearby, close, within reach, accessible, not particularly far away;
- approaching, oncoming, imminent, heading towards one or about to occur (especially of something negative)

Etymology: originally in the first sense, used as an exclamation to attract attention or hail another ship. The extended uses are seen from the nineteenth century, initially restricted to colloquial or humorous contexts.
