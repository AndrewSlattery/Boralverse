228.2: greffour "clerk"

greffour /grɛˈfur/ [gʀɪˈfʊː]
- clerk, functionary, office worker, secretary, registrar, one employed in managing records of e.g. accounts or correspondence;
- scribe, copyist, amanuensis, one who writes out documents or takes dictation by hand;
- pen-pusher, bureaucrat, a petty official preoccupied with procedure and forms;
- (dialect, archaic) scarecrow, a straw effigy placed in a field to deter birds from eating the crops planted there

Etymology: Middle Borlish, originally in legal use for scribes of court, an alteration of synonymous greffer via contemporary French greffier from Medieval Latin graphiārius "scribe, secretary". This is a derivative of graphium "stylus", whence also obsolete gref "office of a court registrar" (which may alternatively be the term's direct origin). The pejorative metaphorical extension the term is seen from the nineteenth century. The agricultural usage is of obscure motivation.
