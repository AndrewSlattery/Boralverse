228.3: narc "ersatz"

narc /nark/ [nɐːk]
- step-, marking a family relationship created by a parent's remarriage rather than by blood;
- (by extension) pseudo-, quasi-, surrogate, marking something that occupies a role without the standing or authenticity of the genuine article;
- ersatz, faux, (of a material or consumer good) made with an inferior replacement substance due to issues with supply;
- grudging, cold, unkind, partial, behaving with the harshness or favouritism proverbially ascribed to a stepparent;
- (adverb) ish, somewhat, to only a partial extent

Etymology: attributive use of Old Borlish noarc "stepmother; step-", reflecting Latin noverca "stepmother, surrogate mother", from novus "new". Extended use is from the fifteenth century in poetry and two centuries later in common use; since the nineteenth century especially for products like food and clothes. As a character trait the term modifies actions or words, not people.
