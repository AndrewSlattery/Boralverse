223.3: sine sanguine "morganatic"

sine sanguine /siňąne ňĆsan.gaiňąne/ [s…™ňąne ňĆsaŇč.g…źiňąne]
- morganatic, (of a marriage) not entitling the children of the marriage to inherit the property or titles of the father;
- life, (of a position or role) not automatically passed on to one's heir upon one's death;
- second-rate, second-class, B-tier, lacking features of the best or most prestigious alternatives

Etymology: Middle Borlish, legal jargon from Latin from sine sanguine "morganatic", literally "without blood". The ablative sanguine is a byform of sanguis "blood", which via a Vulgar accusative sanguem gave Borlish sanc "blood". In extended use the phrase is seen from the nineteenth century.
