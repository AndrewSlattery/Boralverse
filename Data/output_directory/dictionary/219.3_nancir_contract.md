219.3: nancir "contract"

nancir /nanňąts…™r/ [n…źnňąts…™ňź]
- contract, catch, to become infected or afflicted with;
- incur, bring on, meet with, to become affected by (some negative condition or consequence);
- be saddled with, to be required to be in the possession of (something one would rather not have)

Etymology: Middle Borlish, borrowed from medical Latin nancńęscŇć "I contract (an illness)", a non-deponent variant of Classical nancńęscor "I encounter, stumble on". The word is seen in non-medical contexts from the eighteenth century.
