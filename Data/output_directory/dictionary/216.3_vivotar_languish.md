216.3: vivotar "languish"

vivotar /ňĆvi.voňątar/ [ňĆvi.v äňąt…źňź]
- languish, rot, pine away, to live without accomplishing anything;
- get by, struggle, make ends meet, to live in miserable conditions or generally with very limited means

Etymology: early Modern Borlish, borrowed from contemporary French vivoter "to be ill; to languish". This is a diminutive formation from vivre "live", from Latin vńęvŇć "I live".
