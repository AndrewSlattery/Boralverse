214.3: rebateyað "born again"

rebateyað /ˌre.baˈtjaθ/ [ˌʀe.bɐˈtjaθ]
- rebaptised, born again, dedicated anew to a religious identity (often a more strident or demanding version of one's former faith);
- rechristened, renamed, provided with a new name or an entire new identity;
- zealous, fervent, notably dedicated to a newly adopted conviction;
- revamped, rebranded, relaunched, (of a commercial entity or product) given a new public identity

Etymology: past participle of rebateyar "rebaptise, rename", a derivative of bateyar "baptise, christen". This descends from Medieval Latin baptidiō "I baptise", itself via Late Latin baptizō "I dip into water" from Greek βαπτίζω • (baptízō) "I immerse, plunge". Metaphorical extension outside religious contexts is seen from the eighteenth century; use for things as well as people is attested from the twentieth century.
