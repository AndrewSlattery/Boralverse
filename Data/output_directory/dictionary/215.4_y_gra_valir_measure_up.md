215.4: y gra valir "measure up"

y gra valir /i gra vaˈlɪr/ [i gra vɐˈlɪː]
- be up to the challenge, meausre up, to have some quality allowing one to succeed at some difficult endeavour;
- hold one's own, rise to the occasion, to succeed despite adversity;
- have the balls, dare, to possess enough courage or audacity to attempt something;
- matter, count for something, be worth it, to have enough merit to bother considering

Etymology: idiom attested from as early as the fifteenth century in Kigman's poetry, literally "to be worth (the) importance". The verb valir "be worth; should" descends directly from Latin valeō "I have power, I am well". The noun gra "importance, degree, extent" reflects Old Borlish gra "level, sake, weight", which conflates the reflex of Latin gravis "heavy" with that of Latin gradus "step, rank, position".
