214.4: varennastr "underworld"

varennastr /ˌva.rɛˈnastr̩/ [ˌva.ʀɪˈna.stɐ]
- underworld, demimonde, that part of society engaged in crime or vice (often in particular organised crime or prostitution);
- black market, the network of illicit trade of restricted goods;
- underbelly, the hidden or unsavoury side of an insitution or subculture

Etymology: 19th century, pejorative derivation from varen "burrow, warren, lair, sett (underground animal dwelling); slum, favela, shantytown". This was borrowed in the early Middle Borlish period from Norman warenne (compare Parisian guerenne "hunting ground, public park"). This comes either via Frankish from Classical German variō "I defend from, I ward against", or via Gaulish varrennā "fenced area" from Classical Kelt varrā "fencepost" (whence modern Welsh guar "neck").
