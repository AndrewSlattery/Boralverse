230.2: inaltour "elitist"

inaltour /ňĆi.nalňątur/ [ňĆi.n…źlňąt äňź]
- elitist, snob, one who holds high culture to be inherently superior;
- gatekeeper, arbiter, a self-appointment judge of refinement or sophistication;
- shibboleth, marker, status symbol, something otherwise innocuous which marks one as belonging to a rarefied group

Etymology: early modern period, agentive derivation from inaltar "highly esteem, put on a pedestal". This was borrowed from Latin inaltŇć "I raise high; I exalt", from altus "high". Extension to people or things which separate an in-group from everyone else is seen from the early twentieth century.
