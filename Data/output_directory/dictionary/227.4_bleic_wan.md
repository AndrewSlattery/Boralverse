227.4: bleic "wan"

bleic /blik/ [blik]
- wan, pallid, ashen, drained of healthy colour;
- faint, feeble, washed-out, lacking brightness or saturation;
- weak, half-hearted, lacking warmth or conviction;
- bleak, cheerless, desolate, offering little comfort

Etymology: from Old Borlish bleic "pale, whitish", a borrowing of synonymous bleikr in Old Norse, which reflects Classical German blaecas "white". The word was crowded out of neutral usage by blanc "white" in the Middle Borlish period, and now carries contemptuous or unpromising connotations.
