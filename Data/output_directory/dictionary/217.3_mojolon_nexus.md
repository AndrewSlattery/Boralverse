217.3: mojolon "nexus"

mojolon /ňĆmo. íoňąl…Ēn/ [ňĆmo. Ě äňąl…Ēn]
- nexus, hub, interchange, a point on many transit routes and thus where many travellers pass through;
- destination, attraction, a location that draws visitors from afar for an entertaining or otherwise enriching experience

Etymology: 18th century, augmentative derivative of mojol "hub of a wheel, centrepoint". This is itself from Latin modiolus "small cup, hub of a wheel", diminutive of modius, a unit of dry volume. Originally a term of art in cartographic circles, it entered common use with the spread of convoy [rail] networks.
