221.2: zaloc "bail"

zaloc /za틛l톖k/ [za틛l톖k]
- pawn, hock, the state of being held as security for a loan;
- collateral, security, something used to secure a loan;
- bail, a sum of money exchanged for the release of an arrested person as a guarantee of that person's appearance for trial;
- (colloquial) leverage, bargaining chip, an abstract or physical source of advantage over some situation

Etymology: 18th century in underworld slang, borrowed from Willem Dutch zalock, zalogh "pawn". This and related terms originate in zaloh "pawn" in Bohamer [Czech], a descendant of a synonymous zalogo in Classical Slavone. By the twentieth century the term had displaced the offical legalese vad "bail" in all but the most rarefied settings.
