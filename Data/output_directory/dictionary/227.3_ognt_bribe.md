227.3: ognt "bribe"

ognt /ɔjnt/ [ɔint]
- bribe, kickback, sweetener, a payment or favour given to corrupt another's conduct;
- sop, inducement, palliative, a small concession offered to placate or quieten someone;
- (literal, formal) grease, ointment, salve, unguent, a fatty substance used to lubricate or anoint;
- (figurative) unction, flattery, blandishment, soft soap, smooth or unctuous talk meant to ingratiate

Etymology: direct descendant of Latin ūnctus "anointment", nominal derivation from ungō "I anoint, smear with oil". Rarely used for actual anointing substances in the modern language, except in ceremonial contexts. Metaphorical extension to favours and concessions is attested since the early Middle Borlish period.
