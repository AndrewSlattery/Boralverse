220.3: akirtanç "outbreak"

akirtanç /ˌa.kɪrˈtants/ [ˌa.kɪːˈtans]
- sparking, ignition, the act of setting alight with a spark;
- flare-up, outburst, eruption, a sudden intensification in violence or activity;
- outbreak, the sudden spread of a disease (also metaphorically);
- hit, breakout success, a work leading its creator to sudden mainstream success

Etymology: Middle Borlish, noun derivation from akirtar "spark, kindle, ignite". This itself comes from Old Borlish kirte "spark" (modern kirt), probably of onomatopoetic origin. The word has been used of diseases both within an individual and across a population since the seventeenth century, and in the last senses it first appeared in the Good Game period of the late nineteenth century.
