231.4: pendramnougr "headlong"

pendramnougr /ňĆp…õn.dramňąnugrŐ©/ [ňĆp…õn.d Ä…źmňąnu.g…ź]
- head-first, headlong, with the head at the front;
- full throttle, hell for leather, flat out, pedal to the metal, as all get-out, helter-skelter, proceeding at or accelerating to maximum speed;
- rashly, hastily, recklessly, slapdash, without pausing to think anything through;
- (sometimes proscribed) posthaste, immediately, forthwith, pronto, at once, with the minimum possible delay

Etymology: eighteenth century borrowing from Welsh pendramnwgl "head-first, upside-down; in disarray; at loggerheads". This is a concatenation of pen "head", tra "over, across" and mnwgl "neck, throat" attested as an adverb from the thirteenth century. In the last senses from the 1910s, especially in facetious requests.
