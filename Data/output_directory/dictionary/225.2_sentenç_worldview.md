225.2: sentenç "worldview"

sentenç /sɛnˈtɛnts/ [sɪnˈtɛns]
- worldview, ethos, outlook, the framework of values and assumptions through which a person interprets the world;
- (formal) opinion, view, stated position on a particular matter;
- maxim, aphorism, dictum, a pithy saying with moral or didactic force;
- (legal, archaic) sentence, judgement, judgment, the formal pronouncement of a court

Etymology: borrowed from Latin sententia "opinion, judgment; thought", itself a derivative of sentiō "I feel, perceive, think". The term was first attested in the Middle Boral period in juridical contexts. The "maxim" sense flowered in the moralising literature of the early modern period, when collections of aphorisms circulated widely as pedagogical tools.
