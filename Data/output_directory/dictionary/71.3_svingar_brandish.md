71.3: svingar "brandish"

svingar /sv…™nňągar/ [zv…™Ňčňąg…Ďňź]
- brandish, wield, to hold something (especially a weapon) in an ostentatious or threatening manner;
- flourish, wave, show off, to display something ostentatiously

Etymology: Middle Boral borrowing from some Germanic language, most likely Saxon swingen "to swing, wave, brandish", but Merch and Horther have also been suggested.
