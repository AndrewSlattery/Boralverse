216.2: vlangr "fence"

vlangr /ˈvlan.gr̩/ [ˈvlaŋ.gɐ]
- fence, receiver, middleman, someone who buys and sells stolen goods;
- accessory, abettor, a person not present at a crime but bearing some responsibility for it as an assistant or instigator

Etymology: mid-19C in underworld slang, imported from the slightly earlier British form recorded in the Glamour of Deganny. This in turn reflects Suahile [Swahili] valanguri, plural of mlanguri "healer", used in Ongutian [Zanzibari] argot to mean "profiteer, black marketeer". The Borlish term was popularised outside criminal circles by its use in a series of high-profile mob trials in the first decade of the twentieth century.
