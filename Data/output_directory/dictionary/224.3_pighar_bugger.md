224.3: pighar "bugger"

pighar /paˈjar/ [pɐˈʝɐː]
- bugger, top, to penetrate in the course of anal sex;
- (loosely) fuck, to have sex with;
- screw, cheat, ruin, to deceive or to scupper the chances of by underhanded means;

Etymology: disputed. Attested since the eleventh century in late Old Borlish piȝar "bugger", making it the second-oldest vulgarity for intercourse after foutr "fuck" < Latin futuō "I fuck". Possibly the word is a dialectal form of piglar "plunder, pillage"—compare Old Borlish caiȝ "toggle" < Vulgar Latin cavicla "latch". Alternatively, it might derive directly from Latin pēdīcō "I bugger" (although the word has no other descendants).
