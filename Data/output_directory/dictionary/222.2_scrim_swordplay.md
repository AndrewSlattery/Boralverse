222.2: scrim "swordplay"

scrim /xrɪm/ [xʀɪm]
- swordplay, fencing, the art or skill of fighting with blades;
- scuffle, skirmish, clash, a minor or disorganised physical confrontation (also metaphorical);
- banter, repartee, persiflage, rapid witty or combative back-and-forth in conversation

Etymology: early modern borrowing from Tuscan scrima "swordplay", a deverbal from scrimire (also schermire) "fence, fight with swords". This itself was borrowed from Lombard Dutch skirmen or Frankish skirmian "protect, shield", both from Classical German scirmis "pelt; covering, shelter". Extension to verbal sparring is seen from the late eighteenth century.
