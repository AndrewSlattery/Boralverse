129.4: kirt ag madou "catalyst"

kirt ag madou /k…™rt …õj maňądu/ [k…™ňźt …õj m…źňądu]
- catalyst, trigger, spark, inciting incident, a thing or event that sets something (especially something of outsized impact) into motion;

Etymology: idiom attested from the 18C, literally "spark to the kindling". The noun kirt "spark" descends from synonymous kirte in Old Borlish, and is probably onomatopoeic in origin. The noun madou "tinder, kindling" is a borrowing from Middle French (a)madou "lure, bait; kindling, touchwood", a deverbal of amadouer "coax, lure; kindle". This verb is thought to descend via Old Norrish mata "lure, feed" from Classical German *matŇćnam "feed".
