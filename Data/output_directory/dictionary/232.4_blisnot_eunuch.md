232.4: blisnot "eunuch"

blisnot /bliňązn…Ēt/ [bl…™ňązn…Ēt]
- (historical) eunuch, castrato, a castrated man especially as employed in court or harem service;
- gelding, wether, ox, a castrated male animal kept for work or riding;
- third party, honest broker, neutral party, one trusted precisely for having nothing to gain;
- escrow, a contractual arrangment with assets held in trust by an independent agent

Etymology: from Old Borlish blisnod, blestin, belsont (among others) "eunuch, castrated man". This was borrowed from synonymous belisnod in Old English, from the verb belisnian "to castrate". The term's non-metaphorical use for castrated people has been mostly supplanted by local terms such as eunuch or yanŇÜin, outside of fantasy or historical fiction. The financial and mercantile senses are attested from as early as the fourteenth century.
