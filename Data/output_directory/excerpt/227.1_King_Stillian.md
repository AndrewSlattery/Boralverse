227.1: King Stillian

notes in translation on a medieval king.

Stiglan d'Istr Boral sta rey sur Istr Boral Roman ny siecr ogt nuverrem e nouf primal.
Stillian of Borland was king of Roman Borland in the late eighth and early ninth centuries.

L'oc Preu Reyaum d'Istr Boral noncupað aye se grandið des y Dioces Boral dell'asc sou y Roman durant y Mitað-Asc joun, stant denuð un monarchy cos parallel con (e plausibr influïð par) y reyaum petty novel instançað par pouvlanç Germannesc nell'ovest e ny souð d'Istr Boral.
This so-called First Kingdom of Borland had grown out of the sub-Roman Diocese of Boral in the early medieval period, having become a monarchy in tandem with (and possibly influenced by) the new petty kingdoms settled by Germanic peoples in the west and south of the island of Borland.

Com toð y rey d'Istr Boral Roman, Stiglan da pretenjon ig star un prosequent de Victor Rossetus, y parson meg mythic oc ayent bempart l'eiðel uinð posc autortað Roman de starn slognau ny siecr cinq.
Like all kings of Roman Borland, Stillian claimed to be descended from Victor Rossetus, the semi-mythic figure who united much of the land once external Roman authority receded in the fifth century.

Y nom Stiglan es congenið dy parol Latin Hostīliānus, ant bustr tesmogn tan com y nom d'un emperour Roman oc regnant brey d'an 251 N.
The name Stillian descends from Latin Hostīliānus, attested also as the name of a Roman emperor who ruled briefly in 251 N.
