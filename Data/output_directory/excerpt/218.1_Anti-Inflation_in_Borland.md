218.1: Anti-Inflation in Borland

excerpt in translation from the 1975 July 1 edition of the Scavlour (Reflecter), a workdaily paper from Borland which focuses on political news.

Com un gest prefait jaçol, y Minister a Abondanç a la corroulað ig toð y catr claus nell'Act cas Preç de Mansc d'an '15 son satisfait.
In a long-anticipated move, the Ministry for Wealth has confirmed that all four conditions of the '15 Food Prices Act have been met.

Y gemma (⍚), oc intermanent y part fondaminal de moneð Borallesc des an 1925, vil denir preu annexað a tandem destarrið par un scet valent 10⍚.
The gemma (⍚), which has been the base unit of Borlish currency since 1925, is to be first supplemented and eventually replaced with a coin worth 10⍚.

Agathin Gottart a sy pretenjon augtað ig scet e bevis novel valuð par y moneð novel voun entrar n'issar sur y Jorn a David d'an 1977.
Minister Gottart has expressed his intention that new coin and notes denominated in the new currency will enter circulation on David's Day of 1977.

Lonc y catr meis prosequent y minister vil droughanç e noçon recevir dy gentagl sur y nom e l'ensign dy moneð novel, con tan postulaçon por un vouð demosin sur y nom final ern contrdontað.
For the next four months the ministry is accepting public input on the name and designs of the new coinage, although calls for a popular vote on the final name have been rejected.
