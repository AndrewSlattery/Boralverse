228.1: Lachesism (cont.)

continued excerpt in translation from the video 'Lachesism', written by John Koenig.

E tandic ty çavel dien youdað par molir a tell'authicanopoesy soccoðr, ty cour sa noc se tenir calm, e ty boðel famisc por scompigl—
And while your brain goes numb trying to shake off your complacency, your heart can’t sit still, and your gut is hungry for chaos—

lim ayent vars porsief dar a torvegl e corrir pendramnougr ag foy; y dissoluçon de civiltað spiar, eð y vray signfiant descovrir, eð y dehiscenç de toll'autr contemplar.
itching to chase after storms and run headlong into the fire; to watch society break down, and find out what’s truly important, and watch everything else fall away.

L'apocalyps es un entr nossy fancy y primallessem, ma y scop n'es eir colombant vars y fin dy stoir.
The apocalypse is one of the oldest fantasies we have, but it’s not about skipping to the end of the story.

Apar nos lagnvisceu revelaçon, y svellanç dell'oç ja noscon saut ma noc reconnuð:
It's a longing for revelation, a revealing of what we already know but cannot see:

ig nien dell'oc vien cos garantið, e noc sceint es untal "við cojan".
that none of this is guaranteed, and there’s no such thing as “ordinary life”.
