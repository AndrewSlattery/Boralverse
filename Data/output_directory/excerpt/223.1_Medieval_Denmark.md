223.1: Medieval Denmark

notes in translation on Denmark in the medieval period.

Danmarc es un polity peninsoler se situant entr y Mar Norðer e Gravinic.
Denmark is a peninsular polity located between the North and Graven Seas.

Danmarc sta un part dell'eiðel dy pouvr Dannesc durant y Paramontað Dannesc nell'epoc dy Mitað-Asc, dont sorten gazou viking eð accommission fragossaver.
Denmark was part of the homeland of the Danes during the Dane Supremacy in the medieval period, from which viking raids and colonisation projects left.

Y regnour d'Istr Boral eð y bempart de Larry Dannesc pall'oc epoc broucan linaç ant orin ne Danmarc.
The rulers of Borland and much of Dane England during this time were dynasties originating in Denmark.

A mesur dy siecr seç Danmarc intermane sodal dy Concert Dannesc, un union monarchal de stat norðer.
During the sixteenth century Denmark was part of the Dane Concert, a monarchic union of northern states.

Y famigl reyal acommis un gran aðendrment de lorry reyaum pall'annað pres d'an 1560, loy visitant com Albourg.
The royal family undertook a grand tour of their realm in the years around 1560, visiting places like Alburgh.

L'oc sta y period yon Revitalism cof presenç a Danmarc, con interrogaçon alcheðr de scrit Classic.
 It was this period which saw Revitalism reach Denmark, with renewed interrogation of Classical texts.
