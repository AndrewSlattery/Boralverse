209.1: Medieval Mayor

notes in translation on Wilhelm Brüz.

Willaum Brüz (fl. 1225) sta bourgmer ny citað d'Augsbourg tras un dezein ag mendrem ny preu mitað dy siecr treç.
Wilhelm Brüz (fl. 1225) was mayor of the city of Augsburg for at least a decade in the first half of the thirteenth century.

Le sta un amig coram de Paul von Burchadt, y preu Pap alcant aposc dy Sce a Augsbourg, e cossirað es ig y pretenjon peculier de Brüz por sy citað influïsceu certan entrell'idea politic cas religion ny Denonçaçon Paulin d'an 1225.
He was a personal friend of Paul von Burchadt, the future first Pope of the Augsburg See, and Brüz's personal ambitions for his city are thought to have influenced some of the religious policy outlines in the Pauline Denuncations of 1225.

Ny sona de Brüz protagoneyau Hunter Noteworthy y lasc d'an 1961 e dy lengaç Markesc Ant y Govon com Mecchiat Nostr, un dramateyaçon dy cainç dy Secession Teudesc.
Brüz was portrayed by Hunter Noteworthy in the 1961 Merch-language film With the North Star to Guide Us, a dramatisation of the events of the German Secession.

Un çocr magnfeyað de Brüz (oc font noc ayent a alcun descreuçon ben depegnt dy segr, dec no scein nien) deneu fis d'an 1975 a starn dy hal muncever n'Augsbourg.
A magnified statue of Brüz (not based on any extant description or depiction of the man, since none exists) was erected in 1975 outside Augsburg's town hall.
