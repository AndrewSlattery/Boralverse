216.1: Axbane and Commerce

notes in translation on the Borlish city of Axbane, located on the southwestern coast of the island.

Nell'oc epoc, Ausbagn se situant all'un cost dy Stuer Rustig vale immens cas eðegl.
At this time, the location of Axbane on one side of the Rustigh Strait was of immense strategic value.

Ausbagn denoy tandem un entr y citað protagoneyour ny Stadbond, un colluy de citað marcander ny region.
Axbane eventually became one of the star cities of the Stadbund, an association of mercantile cities in the region. 

Cossirað es ig d'an 1400N, y colanç de sdroy tras y havan d'Ausbagn teye may d'un tarç de commarç European deur y Miterran.
The throughput of the Axbane port is thought to have accounted for over a third of European trade outside the Middlesea by 1400N. 

Ny siecr catorç nuverrem caye tan ig l'oc citað Borallesc corrogau un alleyanç cordial cos particuler con Bremen.
The late fourteenth century also saw this Borlish city develop a particularly close relationship with Bremen.

Ny dezein d'an 1850 sceye y lign barric sou mar entr Ausbagn e Texel, un dy primer tal connexion campaner ny mont tras un mar. 
The 1850s saw the Axbane-Texel undersea barric line, one of the world's first such steeplepost connections across the sea.

Nell'oc epoc, Ausbagn sta sodal ny Nou Sar Urban cosogr citað autonom com Londr.
At this time, Axbane was part of the New Urban Mesh along with independent cities like London.
