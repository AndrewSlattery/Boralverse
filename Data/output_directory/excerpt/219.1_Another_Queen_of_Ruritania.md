219.1: Another Queen of Ruritania

continued translation from the beginning of [real-life] book The Henchmen of Zenda by KJ Charles, a 2018 pastiche of the 1894 romp The Prisoner of Zenda.

Y Rey, cos evident noc scroçað par cordougl, er remariðað ant un hast cossirað par y livraç com impolið, all'oc vey an domn lon alcun grad naðusc de pres dy tarmn souðer.
The King, evidently not racked by grief, married again with what sticklers regarded as indecent haste, this time to a woman of no significant birth from near the southern border.

La sta fosc de cavel e ðus de character, e jo presum ig l'aus un vray largeç seyon indignað, cos parçal dec sy mariðaç sta sine sanguine.
She had dark hair and a hell of a temper, and I expect she had a great deal to be angry about, in part because her marriage was morganatic.

Oc a digr, l'istranç gradual entr yoc e femn intermane tan por ig un progenies calscon nolois par engin star heriter dy grad de lorry paðr.
That is to say, the difference in rank between husband and wife was such that any offspring would not be automatically heir to the father’s titles.

Ða stove se dar ne mariðaç an Elphberg, desirað cos starc fos y vajot broucar.
If one had to be married to an Elphberg, one really would want to get the benefits.
