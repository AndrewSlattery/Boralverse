215.1: The Red Man

notes in translation on the horror story The Red Man (originally in Bulgar as Мaжaт вЧервено "The Man in Red").

Y Segr Roug es un cont spagent scaut par Petcov ne Sreddies dy dezein 1830 e jagatteyað un dezein paravant.
The Red Man is a fright tale written by Petcov in 1830s Sreddies [Sofia] and set a decade earlier.

L'oc interman y souc dy Var Theudesc Dovem eð y scadriscenç cogernt dy Reyaum ag Danau.
This is the aftermath of the Second German War and the concomitant disintegration of the Kingdom of the Danaw [Danube].

Y livr abransc y Boxa nou sovran, y ruin se recostroyent de Stamboul, e plusour dy citað ny region ec volent alcant aposc unir por y "Citað Floyan" noncupað stablir.
The book features newly-independent Boxa [Novi Sad], the recovering shell of Stamboul [Istanbul], and many of the cities of the region which would eventually band together to form the so-called "Riverine Cities".

Ny Segr Roug scein heros ec attenent a l'enigma descifrar dy parson dignant Zechariah, un vampyr; l'oc ouvr tannisceu bempart dy jout cas y vampyr, ig inclus for, sanc e jonnovar.
The Red Man has heroes trying to decipher the mysterious figure known as Zechariah, a vampire; this work codified many of the tropes of the vampire, such as fire, blood and butterflies.
