214.1: Hair and Heredity

continued translation from the beginning of [real-life] book The Henchmen of Zenda by KJ Charles, a 2018 pastiche of the 1894 romp The Prisoner of Zenda.

Jo vos hortamin a l'oc signfeyað cossirar.
I invite you to consider what this means.

Por ig un famigl vel cavel rous eð ogl azur retenir lonc dou siecr, n'un eiðel noc verdeyant necun de rous ben de nobr, stovent es ou focment scrutinious ben un mescomprenjon dell'axiom sengr de piver.
For a family to remain red-headed and blue-eyed down two centuries, in a land not filled with redheads or aristocrats, requires either careful breeding or a failure to grasp simple principles of stock management.

Sceint y bustr vecin dy linaç reyal Hapsburg, dont y caðen ancestral visnous rasgattovau par un lunatic lasdr ant un granmaðr stant oðig sy morrig, se poð demar ig l'Elphberg volois lorry costumn de mariðaç entr cosvour recuidar cos coðelosessem, ma com l'ec fougl voun l'illustrar, inacavabr es un aristocrat Rurtan addocir de nien.
Given the nearby example of the Hapsburg royal family, whose twisted family tree ended in a wretched lunatic whose grandmother was also his aunt, one might think the Elphbergs would have considered their habit of cousin-marriage more carefully, but as these pages will show, you can’t tell a Ruritanian aristocrat anything.

Un trenteinastr prell'inceuçon de nossy cont, y Rey Rudolf IV a Rurtagn mariðau sy cosvour preu stemn, oc cavel rous eð ogl azur parsolent contr sy defait de nis, çavel e menton.
Some thirty years before our story starts, Ruritania’s King Rudolf IV married his first cousin, who made up in red hair and blue eyes what she lacked in stamina, brains, or chin.

La luy nastisceu un heir morvaç eð obægr, tan Rudolf (un hom sur dou nell'oc stoir foudent dign Rudolf, e jo poð for deliquy vos souvr), e prose l'egreyeu, ayent sy deyaç complið.
She gave him a sickly brat of a son, also Rudolf (every second person in this damned tale is called Rudolf, for which I can only apologise), and promptly expired, her duty done.
