203.1: Saxony

notes in translation on a German polity.

A mesur dy Scon Tetrarchy, y Reyaum a Saxony intermane un stat vassaler majour nell'Empir Teudesc administrað des Bardovic, e plusour ne lour dynasty de Hohenbrocken regnaurn sull'empir enter.
During the Second Tetrarchy, the Kingdom of Saxony was a major vassal state of the German Empire administered from Bardwick, and many of their Hohenbrocken dynasty ruled the empire outright.

Y Reyaum a Saxony deneu toccað par y sfondranç dy Scon Tetrarchy ny siecr treç.
The Kingdom of Saxony was affected by the collapse of the Second Tetrarchy in the thirteenth century.

Y Stadbond smargent de citað marcander ag norð secedeu eð abascau Saxony vars sell'eðegl amont a Magdbourg e Lepcy.
The nascent Stadbund of mercantile cities in the north seceded and reduced Saxony to its lands upstream in Maidburg and Lepzi.

Cossirað es ig Saxony a y plurem dy vajot vancað des y Var Theudesc Primer; ny souc den y polity gourna may d'eiðel a alcot seyon posseut n'aucun hour de sy histoir.
Saxony is considered to have profited most from the First German War; in its aftermath the polity controlled more territory than it had at any point in its history.

Durant y siecr novoç, y Çaucr Azur Marian (oc instaurað a Lepcy) tarromoy commouð vars reform cas ley de heritanç e de dreit slecçonal.
In the nineteenth century, the Blue Marian Society (which was founded in Lepzi) spearheaded movements for reform to inheritance and suffrage laws.
