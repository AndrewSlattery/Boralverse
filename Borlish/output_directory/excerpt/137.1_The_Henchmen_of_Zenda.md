137.1: The Henchmen of Zenda

excerpt in translation from the [real-life] book The Henchmen of Zenda by KJ Charles, a 2018 pastiche of the 1894 romp The Prisoner of Zenda.

L'yarn parmane com un madarc obstroint tras semman perpetu e monoton, e soulor i fey exeunt pre un cocoro.
Winter lingered like an unwanted policeman for endless monotonous weeks, and then departed overnight.

Nos se sveilau an jorn affaçað de lag verdeyant parsemnað d'ec borjon de flour, cant orruðe l'eiðel enter fos sy quidel yettant a fragnent a vivaçtað a coup.
We woke one day to green meadows dotted with the beginnings of flowers, as the entire country seemed to throw off its blankets and burst into life at once.

Aucel ençaurn a cantar, agnel apparisceurn des zefr, y paç dy Castel a Zenda fo smuïð, eð y reu de my við denoy trent veg convoluðessem.
Birds sang, lambs appeared from nowhere, the peace of the Castle of Zenda was shattered, and my existence became thirty times as complicated.

Dell'eç dou vertað derran, com de tan cainç, jo no poð for blasm jair a Rupert de Hentzau.
For the latter two facts, as for so many things, I can only blame Rupert of Hentzau.

Bon Deu, comoscon Hentzau illuminar sur fougl?
My God, how to convey Hentzau on the page?

No se poð l'acavar cos honest neðan contrvenir all'Act cas Ediçon Obscen.
One could not do it honestly without contravening the Obscene Publications Act.
