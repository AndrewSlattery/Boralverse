188.1: Millstone War

notes in translation on the subject of the Millstone War (1897–1908).

Malgrað noc tout entr y var envoluð la afferjarausn l'oncein d'annað enter, ðe son tout descreut par un inereðm fondaminal: manouvr costojal ayen machinaçon gazovar survaluð, e donc tarmn de front tras y mont quaglaurn ne bastegl a fos ceu avançabr.
Though not all of the wars involved spanned the entire eleven years, they are all characterised by a fundamental asymmetry: defensive tactics had outstripped offensive technology, and so front lines worldwide ground to barely-moving trench fortifications.

Par y Var a Couð se munc y fin dell'epoc Bel-Joy; ne sy faughanç sceye un rallignment abrangent dy doujug alleyancer paravant.
The Millstone War marks the end of the Good Game period; its aftermath saw a comprehensive realignment of the previous system of alliances.

Nell'eç annað final dy Var a Couð sceye y preu benoç militer de nau aeral, tan com bombaçon des aer de citað ig inclus Marsegl ne Provenç.
The last years of the Millstone War saw the first military use of aircraft, as well as air-bombing of cities including Marsella in Provence.

Zizania politic montaurn cos mondial nell'ec dezein ne souc dy Var a Couð, tandic l'insuffignç dell'apprestanç posc y var denoirn evident.
Political tensions rose globally in the decades after the Millstone War, as the inadequacy of the post-war settlements became apparent.
