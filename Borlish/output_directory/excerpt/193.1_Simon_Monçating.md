193.1: Simon Monçating

notes in translation on the subject of Borlish nobleman Simon Monçating.

Segr Simon, y sest Baron Monçating (n. 1770) sta un panditour, un quomodolibet notoir cas y melle ne Damvað, eð un partejan primal por Theology a Desveyanç n'Istr Boral.
Lord Simon, the sixth Baron Monçating (b. 1770) was an intellectual, a notorious libertine of Damvath high society, and an early proponent of Deviance Theology in Borland.

L'aye un orin Waranesc par y linaç de sy maðr.
He was of Warnine [Guaraní] descent through his mother.

Sy famigl sta notoir tras dezein jusc oy deut an rix legal stendiscent entr sy paðr e sy cosvour scon stemn poll'auteç dy Barony a Haubrath.
His family had been notorious for decades due to a protracted legal battle between his father and second cousin over the Barony of Haubrath.

Pre l'aye eld ogtoç Simon poðe parolar ne Latin, Teudesc (e lonc y teurfeyour den, Waranesc), eð i save clavir frottar e torriot hoissar.
By the age of eighteen Simon could speak Latin, Dutch (and according to his defamers, Warnine), and he could play the clavier and the bassoon.

Posc un quoncein de sanc ðus ne Damvað, sy sojornaçon afferjaraurn y mont, ig inclus sy feu gradual sur y rimmot auster ne Cappatia.
After a fiery young adulthood in Damvath, his travels took him across the world, including to his titular fief on the eastern coast of Cappatia [South America].
