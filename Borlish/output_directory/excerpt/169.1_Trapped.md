169.1: Trapped

excerpt in translation from the children's tale of adventure Joke Tawyer and the Emeralds in the Castle, written (originally in Merch) in 1964 by an unidentified member of the Kilshaw writers' collective.

Jo pogn y pujoncr des y moð moglastr par un resmargenç baurous d'energy.
I snatch the knife from the damp earth with a desperate surge of energy.

Sy pis suet m'interman un hormesc consolant ny scompigl.
Its familiar weight is a comforting anchor in the chaos.

Y lamn fay trenc mordent par y cabr gros dy sar cos fagl, cant casc coup hastous spein un cascat a cort se cardonant com y torjon d'un sarpent.
The blade bites through the net's coarse fibers with ease, as each swift cut releases a cascade of ropes that unwind like the coils of a serpent.

Jo me sent n'impisantour tras demy de pousson pre jo plombet par gringr lon graç; my corp liesc vars y makisaç con martelment oc mell'os peyaçant eð y respir volant des my poumon.
I feel weightless for half a heartbeat before I plummet in a graceless tumble, my body crashing into the undergrowth with a thud that jars my bones and knocks the breath from my lungs.

Fougl e bac jagotn mell'accostur, eð un stig parfignt fay gavlot ne my spal, ma no scey alcun hour por commorar ne malestr.
Leaves and twigs snag my clothes, and a sharp pain lances through my shoulder, but there's no time to linger in discomfort.
