43.1: Mysarnos

excerpt in translation from 1943 history book The Mysarnos Problem: the Enigma of an Inconvenient Codex, written by classicist Theodora Riphæna.

Ma neðan carrug mettr avant naumagl, retorneu ag citað yon a nossy racont sy commet.
But lest we get ahead of ourselves, let us return to the city where our saga begins.

Y citað a Mysarnos es l'ançan cafloy ny Principaltað de Mystra, un stat mordether stant vassal sou Rom des y siecr onç fin a sy dissolution com issent parmy ci dy Rix Romannesc dy siecr settoç.
The city of Mysarnos is the former capital of the Principality of Mystra, a Roman mordether vassal state from the eleventh century until its dissolution as a result of the Romantic Wars in the seventeenth.

L'oc nom es tan denuð cos janual l'ig benoçað por y bourg grec vecin ja sceint (stant tardessem corporalisað cos solemn com un muncef de Mysarnos) connuð nell'asc antic com Pylos.
The name has retrospectively also been applied to the nearby preexisting Greek town (which was later officially incorporated as a municipality of Mysarnos) known in antiquity as Pylos.

A sy sommet ny siecr seç sta Mysarnos un mojol por y commouð revitalist, ant un pouvlanç teint pres trey murðon.
At its height in the sixteenth century Mysarnos was a hub for the Revitalist movement, with a population of nearly thirty thousand.
