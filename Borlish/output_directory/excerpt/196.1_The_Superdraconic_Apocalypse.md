196.1: The Superdraconic Apocalypse

notes in translation on the subject of an event in the geologic record.

L'Apocalyps Surdraconic fornisc nom ag cug d'un sax cisjovial ayent se prodoit cos approximað deur segsant seg cent murðon d'annað, cos plausibr durant y prinvir norðer.
The Superdraconic Apocalypse refers to the fall of a asteroid from the asteroid belt approximately sixty-six hundred myriad years ago, probably during the northern spring.

Ne sy souc sceye l'extirpaçon de tre cart tanc entr toll'orn d'animal, toð dragon abrangent sauf ig l'ancestr d'aucel sceint.
It was followed by the eradication of roughly three-quarters of all animal species, including all dinosaurs bar the ancestors of modern birds.

Y nom dell'oc cainç, oc orðenjað ny siecr novoç com un dob apponmenter (dezein plusour pre scerra y descovart d'evidence concret dy quinment) por l'extirpaçon ny gravatoir reliquyer motivar, jay soumoin a sy zon—temporal ag fin, e donc chthonic a dessur, dell'Asc Draconic (2.5–0.66 gran murdon d'annað paravant). 
The name of this event, coined in the nineteenth century as a hypothetical (several decades before evidence of the impact was actually discovered) to explain the extinction in the fossil record, refers to its location—temporally at the end of, and therefore geologically on top of, the Draconic Age (2.5–0.66 great myriad years ago).
