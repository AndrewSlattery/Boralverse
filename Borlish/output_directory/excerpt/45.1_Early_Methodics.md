45.1: Early Methodics

excerpt in translation from 1999 scholastic quire The Grimmer Hall: Developments in Early Methodics (originally published in Cambrick as Łys y Grymrageð: Dasplygad nhy Methodig Kysevin), written by New Leudong historian and methodist Cai Sonquo during his tenure at the University of Mehannin (Brisbane).

Obligað no's un sautel lonc demay cas figuration por y despley ondatim benoçar por regest exactessem augrimar dy gyr de corp cisjovial.
It does not take too great a conceptual leap to use the waveform display to compute more accurate records of the gyres of cisjovial bodies.

Vray, pre y siecr novoç tost ja encontrau nos prefaction magn tranq de conjonction planeter Ixion envount ny scaunç dell'astronom ambrojan Fabian Mostàf,
Indeed, already by the early 1800s we see very precise predictions of planetary conjunctions involving Ixion [Ceres] in the writings of Ambrosian astronomer Fabian Mostàf,

granfigl dy mathematurg Raymond al Mustafa oc meðes ayent aïðað a y fondamen de methodic defassar deur cinquant annað.
grandson of the mathematician Raymond al Mustafa who himself had helped develop the foundations of methodics a half-century earlier.

Nell'oc asc er Mostàf laurant all'observatoir de Paratzon ne Brasil auster e noc obstroint y sar campaner parvasiscent se cous progression,
At this time Mostàf was working at the observatory at Paratzon in eastern Brasil [Newfoundland] and despite advancements in the spreading steeplemesh,

collaboration tras l'ocean atlantic remanen lent, descovart plusour sceint fait cos istrað ny Novomont e Vetomont y dou.
collaboration across the Atlantic remained slow, with several discoveries made separately in the Novomund and Vetomund alike.
