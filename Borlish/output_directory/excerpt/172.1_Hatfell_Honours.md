172.1: Hatfell Honours

notes in translation on the subject of a coveted artistic accolade.

Honour de Hatfell son veðrdon plusour soupleyað de posc y siecr veintem primal a artejan ne sce divers y Collujon Drengoçan hamtant (e, posc ig l'oç alleyanç-federaç sfondrau d'an 1961, l'entertað de Gal e l'eç Istr Norðovester).
Hatfell Honours refer to several prizes awarded since the early twentieth century to artists in various fields across the Drengot Collusion (and, after that alliance-federacy collapsed in 1961, across all of Gaul and the Northwestern Isles [Ireland, GB, Borland]).

Y dou Honour ne literatur e ne dram son abrangið par y cougl original de veðrdon, cant novel art ern admis tan recent com y dezein 2010.
The Honours in literature and drama are among the original set of prizes, while new arts have been admitted as recently as the 2010s.

L'ec Honour prenn y nom de Hatfell, un toun norðer ny stat citaðer de Londr jos y tarmn contr Marclont stant y mojol d'un communtað d'autour parnommað ny siecr novoç.
The Honours are named for Hatfell [Hatfield, Hertfordshire], a northern town in the city-state of London near the border with Markland which was home to a collective of renowned authors in the nineteenth century.

D'an 1918, y scrittour Dorotha Quistack gajannau un Honour de Hatfell por un entr sell'adolescenç.
In 1918, the writer Dorotha Quistack won a Hatfell Honour for one of her coming-of-age novels.
