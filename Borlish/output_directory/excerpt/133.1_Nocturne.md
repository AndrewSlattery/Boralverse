133.1: Nocturne

excerpt from Nocturne; or, the Dividends of Vice, a 1978 work of historical fiction written by Kentish author Aleris Houghlismond (pronounced /ˈa.lə.ʀɪs ˈhoːlz.mən/).

Y peyan dessou de mell'azig stan sdruçollabr ag ploy dy crevoscr, relougen com argent deborsellað.
The cobbles beneath my boots were slick with the night’s rain, glistening like pickpocketed silver.

Y lampader vacillan cos febr e lançan ombr fruyant vars mur assombrið par suicr.
The lampposts flickered weakly and cast dancing shadows on soot-dark walls.

L'aer pene cos lort, parvasið con y fler a hum moglastr e con y fum des murðon a goðou.
The air was heavy, thick with the scent of damp earth and the smoke of a thousand chimneys.

Un carrot passau broyent tandic sy rouð clacquaurn sur y pieðr jagottað eð aug moðer souvolau den all'orlaç de my jacquot.
A carriage rattled by, its wheels clattering over the uneven stones and splashing muddy water onto the hem of my coat.

Jo fey un soçavau de maldicçon ma pausau noc a laupar—stove alcun hour despardr.
I cursed under my breath but didn’t break stride—there was no time to waste.

Jo poðe ja l'eç murmuranç lontan de voç oïr com j'aye spien, eç interront par y sounç sporadic de ricottanç heloidous.
I could already hear the distant murmur of voices I was hoping for, punctuated by the occasional burst of raucous laughter.
