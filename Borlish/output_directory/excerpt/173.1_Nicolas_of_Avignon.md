173.1: Nicolas of Avignon

notes in translation on the subject of Nicolas of Avignon (Provincial Nicolà s Avignon /ni.koˈlɔ sa.viˈɲũ/, fl. 1347).

Nicolas d'Avignon sta un architect Borgond parnommað.
Nicolas of Avignon was a renowned Burgund architect.

L'es cos primer æternað desig i condugheu y reconstroinç eð y spanjon dy Cathedral de Sant Sophia ne Stamboul, y citað caver nell'Empir Roman Dovem.
He is principally remembered for leading the renovation and expansion of Saint Sophia's Cathedral in Stamboul, the capital city of the Second Roman Empire.

Rumorað es ig sy benoç de lodrignað experiencer por y dom central basfeyar ayen y corp principal enter dy cathedral rent precartaðer cos minuscr.
His use of experimental techniques in supporting the central dome are alleged to have rendered the entire main body of the cathedral slightly unstable.

Deut all'oc i dign parveg e cos joculant y dobanç de "l'architect dy ruin dell'Empir", la sceint y sfondranç tragic dell'edifiç a mesur d'un ceremony de coronnaç aval de tre cent annað ayent gran part ag dynasty imperial occeut (jos larc cent ec may) e l'insorgenç akirtað volent l'Empir rasgattovar jug.
For this reason he is sometimes jocularly referred to as the "architect of the Empire's demise", given the tragic collapse of the building during a coronation ceremony three hundred years later that killed most of the imperial family (and hundreds more) and sparked the unrest that would end the Empire for good.
