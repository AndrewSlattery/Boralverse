103.1: Graveyard Fiddlers

Y viol cemtir intermann un jout cyclant ny cont a pouvr borallesc.
The graveyard fiddlers are a recurring motif in the folklore of Borland. 

Postulað es ig il sein gast a mort sont ovittað con y broit enigmatic e lontan de baðr viol oïð cos particular jos cemtir eð all'hour prell'ort sol.
They are said to be death spirits responsible for the mysterious sound of string instruments in the air heard especially near cemeteries and in the hour before dawn.

Lorry melody son cos usual descrit com mogn e dolorous.
Their melodies are usually described as low-pitched and mournful.

Orruð y viol cemtir sein un costum peculier n'Istr Boral, dec ustol pouvlancer local no son abrangent d'alcun figur parallel cos ovart.
The fiddlers appear to be a tradition peculiar to Borland, as neighbouring folk traditions do not include any obviously analogous figures.

Il son ottem reconnuð allors lonc ig il forn svoccað ny melodram Al Igne (Ale in Fiamme) par Chiarina Bruno.
They are most known abroad from their inclusion in opera Wings Afire (Ale in Fiamme) by Chiarina Bruno.

Disc l'oyeus y cont dy viol cemtir des sy fraðr, oc ayent abattuð a cost de soldart borallesc a mesur dy Var Theudesc Dovem.
She is said to have heard the tale of the graveyard fiddlers from her brother, who fought alongside Borlish soldiers in the Second German War.
