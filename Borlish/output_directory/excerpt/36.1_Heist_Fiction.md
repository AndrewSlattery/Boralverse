36.1: Heist Fiction

excerpt from the Borland vicine mesh distributed library pages on masquira romance.

Nell'eç ouvr a Carvallo—l'oc preu livr no stant for y primer parmy un rig de doçan—ern introdoit plusour dy qualtað natural por un racont masquiral, e sell'ouvr lou accomblaurn primer accougl com untað cohernt.
It was Carvallo's works—this first book being only the first in a dozen-strong series—which introduced many of the essential properties of a masquira tale, and first presented them together as a coherent whole.

Com bustr, Jalico forgau pres solter y noçon dy romanç kevlar ant heros deyent un sonc preçous accarr a mesur d'un vellisqment convoluð straccanç e delusion partenent;
For example, Jalico almost singlehandedly creates the conceit of the kevler in which our protagonist must obtain a valuable item by means of a complicated scheme involving trickery and deception; 

nos reconnoscm y parol meðes nel y jout sarvir durant certan hour, dec un inceuçon borallesc aïr.
note that the word itself would not be applied to the trope for some time, as it is of Borlish origin. 

Y sonc mout un jovagl, se poð stimar ig masquira va parallel con y costum cathayan veðerrem de fabr tapsue (pig).
Often an item of jewellery, masquira is said to run parallel to the older Cathayan tradition of tapsue (magpie) tales.
