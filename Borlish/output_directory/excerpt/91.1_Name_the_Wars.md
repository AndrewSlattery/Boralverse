91.1: Name the Wars

extract in translation from 2006 Borlish trevold Eç Abat Enonçar (Name the Wars in English), which blends elements of the masquira and housebound enigma genres

Sur Segr Hendric Cortis au stað y cur fornið por y figl orphan de sy fraðr postiressem candon n'aye Baðot for dou meis eld.
Sir Hendric Cortis had been left with the care of his youngest brother’s orphaned child when Baðot was just two months old. 

Segr Hendric e sell'amig e vecin insevrabr Capitan Gafr ayen a catr man y scuir macquað,
Sir Hendric and his inseparable friend and neighbour Captain Gafr had raised the boy between them,

tras annað lorry parrinment vars region divers n'Africa e Chrysia abrejant por poðir remanir cas Baðot tras casc stað candon i repaðre de schol.
for years curtailing their trips to far-flung regions across Africa and Chrysia so that they could be there each summer when he returned from school. 

L'aye pluottað n'apponent ig confraðreç fagl lon convolution sou ciel azur borallesc sta un step ny scagl a natur.
He had grown up assuming that easy, uncomplicated companionship under blue Borlish skies was the natural order of things. 

Sceint oy, posc un biennial brey parmy fraðr de camp denovant vars sell'apocalyps coram meðes, l'ig parisce Eden soluð.
Now, after a brief two years among brothers-in-arms ending in his own private apocalypse, it seemed a lost Eden.
