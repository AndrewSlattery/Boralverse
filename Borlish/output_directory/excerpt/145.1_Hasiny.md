145.1: Hasiny

notes in translation on the polity of Hasiny.

Hasiny collint y polity de Taisca nell'ovest, e sy citað caver es Nadacou, oc se situant tras y rif dy Bahassassin.
Hasiny borders the polity of Taisha [~Texas] to the west, and its capital city is Nadacow, which lies on the Bahassassin [Mississippi].

Y nom Hasiny es congenið des y federaç parey nommað, oç autr annað abrangent d'eiðel sceint oy sou y gournanç de Hasiny e Taisca.
The name Hasiny derives from the federacy of the same name, which once extended across lands which are now part of Hasiny and Taisha.

Y lengaç administraçonal ne Hasiny son Jaugta (Jahtá, y lengaç Mendevan parolabr par y majortað dy pouvlanç) eð y gendr vecin de Cambresc (discrepant a façon signfeyant deur y vernacr de Cambresc Brittan ben Chrysian).
The languages of administration in Hasiny are Jaughta (Jahtá, the Mendevan language spoken by a majority of the populace) and the local variety of Welsh (which differs in important ways from the dialects of British or Chrysian Welsh).

Hasiny sta l'orin de kiveury com spaiðoir de romanç, dont y scop eye a un bucol rustr depegntar con emphas a peyer.
Hasiny was the homeland of the herdtale genre of fiction, which sought to depict a rural idyll with a focus on animal husbandry.
