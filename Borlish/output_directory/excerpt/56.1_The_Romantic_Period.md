56.1: The Romantic Period

excerpt in translation from Lugnt Ros Sur Ont Lugnt (Rosy Dawn on Glittering Waves), written by amateur historian Descard Ausogn in 1929.

L'eç gran accommission corrogationnal descrein y liesc romannesc, con tan l'eç dezein nolen l'ig stikettanç accarr pre un siecr may.
These great infrastrusture projects characterise the Romantic period, although these decades would not gain that appellation for another century.

L'oc nom es primer benoçað posc y Rix Democratic, candon y mitað nuverrem ny siecr settoç er stimað, lon raçonnabiltað, stant un final d'asc aure cas reloðeç e civilisation.
The name is first used after the Democratic Wars, when the latter half of the seventeenth century was seen, nonsensically, as the last golden age of refinement and civilisation.

Defagl es comparaçon noc fair entrell'eç stat insoler e querellant n'Europ ny dezein 1660 e, com bustr, y ci gran veyaç geoscopic de mac Kellot eð al-Casmi ny siecr derran, ben l'envou Novomondin replenið ny sevent.
It is hard not to contrast these insular, squabbling European states of the 1660s with, say, the great geoscopic voyages of mac Kellot and al-Cazmi of the previous century, or the renewed Novomundine interest of the next.

Nentamen, nell'oc asc es ig poum nos y cart modern e suetessem vistar ençant a smargr tras Europ.
Nonetheless, it is in this time that we can see the familiar modern map begin to emerge across Europe.
