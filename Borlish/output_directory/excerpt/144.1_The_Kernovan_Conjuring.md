144.1: The Kernovan Conjuring

notes in translation on the subject of the Kernovan Conjuring.

Y Conjuraçon Cornovan sta un collaboraçon entr y Reyaum a Dorogn Massem e Roger Drengot, Duc a Normandy por y pretenjon dy Duc sur y thron de Cantuer appoyar. 
The Kernovan Conjuring was a collaboration between the Kingdom of Greater Devon and Roger Drengot, Duke of Normandy to press the Duke's claim to the throne of Kent.

Cossirað es l'oc vellisqment com y caus y proismessem por ig y Preu Empir Drengoçan inceveus tras Gal eð Albion souðer.
This plot is considered the proximate cause for the creation of the First Drengot Empire across Gaul [France] and southern Albion [Great Britain].

Y Conjuraçon sta accommis d'an 1029, ne souc dy mort de Rey Rodolf de Cantuer.
The Conjuring was carried out in 1029, following the death of King Rodulf of Kent.

Cantuer sou Rodolf regnant jaçol aye se monfeyað, ant dominanç reconnuð sur y Vessax diminoit (con tan noc inkerellað par Dorogn) e sur Essax ny norð.
Kent under the long-reigning Rodulf had prospered, with recognised supremacy over the rump Wessex (albeit not uncontested by Devon) and over Essex to the north.

Posc ogt dezein de manovranç politic, Rodolf parmane ag sougl de mort eð y succession no sta bogr certan.
After eight decades of political manoeuvring, Rodulf lingered on his deathbed and the succession was deeply uncertain.
