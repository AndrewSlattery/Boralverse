134.1: Lachesism

excerpt in translation from the video 'Lachesism', written by John Koenig.

Tras un murðon d'annað, nos eu y ciel sgardað e se cleunfeyað des hougr.
For a million years, we’ve watched the sky and huddled in fear.

Ma scey calscon jug te reconnosc tu ant un appoy tacent por y torvegl.
But somehow you still find yourself quietly rooting for the storm.

Com ða un part de tey nole plu attendr e t'entrovar candon vole y mont sfondrar par lot, par doum, par y volontað divin, pres a lou defeyar a ty vuscment soupleyar.
As if a part of you is tired of waiting, wondering when the world will fall apart by lot, by fate, by the will of the gods, almost daring them to grant your wish.

Ma veram poð tu vuscar auvorn alcot, dec við interman un joy de hasart, e casc jorn passant es un autr reunç dy blad.
But really, you can wish all you want, because life is a game of chance, and each passing day is another flip of the coin.  

Ineluïbr es tu ignosc y parsou vray dell'oc við.
You can’t help but take this life for granted.

Tell'ogl se habitun ag colour dy mur e tell'oregl tornn cur deur y jacas, com ða ty corp es attenent a y mont oç teyon saut parcolar apart. 
Your eyes adjust to the colour of the walls and your ears tune out the chatter, as if your body’s trying to filter out the world as you know it.
