143.1: Prehistory Curriculum

excerpt from the 1981 Report towards a Normal Teaching for Children at Mantleschool, a public report from the Ministry for Schools within Borland's contemporary anthracite administratration, which was intended to standardise the education of children up to the age of twelve.

Posc il ain schol lajarn complið, val ig stuðant broucn un recordaçon mozað ag mendrem cas y defas des prell'aubon de scrittur jusc l'epoc dy Scon Tetrachy, con tan casc schol son hortaminað a them recendessem admettr lonc y bustr d'envou vecin.
Upon completing mantleschool, pupils should have at least a surface-level understanding of the development from prehistory until the time of the Second Tetrarchy, although individual schools are encouraged to include more recent material as it relates to vicine matters.

Masctighað es ig l'ec them sein abrangent dy costumn ag toun ben muncef local, aglon cainç diçr (battagl, descovart eð aval) ayent avenuð cos veçin.
This may include the traditions of the local village or quarter, or else notable events (battles, discoveries, etc) which occurred nearby.

Enombrað secuð, val ig stuðant recievn y cæros por ec splorar:
Taken in order, pupils should have the opportunity to learn about:

scamn de migraçon vars Istr Boral jusc e par y Kelt Albic
waves of migration into Borland up to and including the Albic Kelts

ðar primal eð y hopr ne pieðr effojað ny tombr a Revel (comparað con y plaçment Orcadesc a Scarreð ottem mantenuð)
early farming and the stoneware found in the Revel mounds (compared to the better-preserved Orkney site at Scarreth)

sojornaçon, conveyaçon e machinaçon, abrangent ag commarç obligað por eramn, l'avent aval de far, eð y costroinç dy labyrinth a Çadrosc
travel, transport and technology, including the trade necessary for bronze, the later introduction of iron, and the construction of the Çadrosc labyrinth

y doujug entr tribu ne castr colliner nell'epoc pre Rom e lorry cultur cas autortað, soumoin jaint vars art, ensignment e jovagl ja sceint
the intertribal system of hill forts in the pre-Classical period and the culture around authority, with reference to extant art, sculpture and jewellery
