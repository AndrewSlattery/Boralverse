181.1: Political Address

excerpt in translation from 2023 diplomatic thriller Follow Suit (originally in Borlish as Pengovin Pardr "Losing at Bezique"), written by author Jan Bervisson (better known for host roles on several TV and radio shows).

"Houm", ençau y directour, oc clugnant sou y lamp ny scenoir.
"Uh," the president began, blinking under the studio lights.

"Lonc y contris derran des y foncçondry, l'oc droughanç porçonmenter se tien fagl entr y tarmn dy Cartr. Y grinment de rebondisc dy Minister a Feu indic cos clar ig scerraun meglfacçon cas aðoinç a lonc mesur."
"According to the agency's latest report, this fiscal intervention remains well within the Charter's terms. The impact review from the Minstry for Land clearly indicates future improvements in long-term growth."

Jo le remarcau cant y Scavin Yara se commoveu, ant un min illeibr. Plausibr i sta a desaiç, noc obstroint i s'alignaus ac cos usual.
I noticed Councillor Yara shift in her seat, her expression unreadable. She was likely concerned, despite her typical alignment.

Le dereðr, y reu de novel colau par ouç tras y mur: racont d'un kerel cas collujon de hamtaçon e dell'evolganç de pistol des l'ambit n'Ausbagn.
Behind her, the newsstream scrolled silently across the wall: stories of a housing compact dispute and leaked correspondence from the Axbane office.

Un jurist quidam oc l'administraçon astuçant cos actual s'entrjey; par sy vagscrit soutil i digna Vellamn.
Some attorney currently advising the administration spoke up; his unobtrusive nameplate read Vellamn.

"Nossell'empory es monfeyant, or zizania entr Feu e Famigl apportn ja lorry vandy. Scey bustr tesmogn d'irruniscabiltað—stant parcanç outrley."
"Our economy is growing, but conflict between Land and Family does present issues. There’s evidence of irregularities—possibly criminal."

Un scavour jos ados hellovaçau, "Toll'oc no's for plouçastr d'annað a slecçon ou?"
A journalist near the back called out, "Isn’t this just election-year theatre?"
