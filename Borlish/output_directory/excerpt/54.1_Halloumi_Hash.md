54.1: Halloumi Hash

translated recipe from 1880 mealbook Pasti Mezziterreni (Middlesea Meals), written by world-traveller Josforo Sant'Angelo.

Primer dragm ougl raccaldar n'un frigsoir lað e tatover frigsar sur foy preuç un cart hour, mout toglað (fin a y mornç se crostn eð y carn se molfin).
First heat some oil in a broad pan and fry the tattover over gentle fire for a quarter-hour, often stirred (until the pieces crispen and the flesh becomes soft).

Bulb de ceul eð y pevr jarras affigr e ci cocinar longhessem naðusc.
Add in the stalk-onion bulb and the jarraspepper and cook a little longer.

Mistur souseïr, ougl ancour raccalder ny frigsoir, eð y kes frigsar dessur jusc aure.
Subside the mix and heat more oil in the pan, on which fry the cheese until golden.

Tatover e kes condensar eð envouvr cos total tandic aromar con sal e pievr sec ne calscon quantað casc quemant.
Bring together the tattover and cheese and fully involve while including salt and drypepper in whichever amount suits.

Farrago porçonnar ne bol covauclið e ci retenir jos y foy, soulor toð ou coroccar sur y frigsoir savorað e ci frigsar volonter.
Divide the farrago by covered bowls and hold near the fire, then crack the eggs into the seasoned pan and fry as desired.

Cos final casc ou mettr dessur dy desc eð y ligt dy ceul fair scadrir atras.
Finally place the eggs atop each dish and scatter with the rest of the stalk-onion.
