131.1: Catrin of Kernow

collected notes in translation on the subject of Catrin of Kernow.

Catrin de Cornou (m. 1233) sta un parson influyent n'Istr Boral durant l'Empir Mitað-Asc.
Catrin of Kernow (d. 1233) was an influential figure in Borland during the Medieval Imperia.

Catrin de Cornou sta y sour antir de Rey Alan VI de Magn Dorogn, oç y Rallignment de Dorogn accommettent d'an 1225.
Catrin of Kernow was the elder sister of King Alan VI of Greater Devon, who orchestrated the Realignment of Devon in 1225.

Lorrell'yam sta Damian y Saif, y preu Pap (ne Rom) stant sleut des entrell'orn religious y Brethin.
Their uncle was Damian the Wise, the first (Roman) Pope to be elected from among the Brethin religious order.

D'an 1292 sceye y chronic Y Við a Catrin de Cornou scaut, y stoir detaglant de sy við joun, sy mariðaç con un nobr Borallesc e sell'assassinat d'an 1233. 
In 1292 the chronicle The Life of Catrin of Kernow was written, detailing her early life, marriage to a Borlish nobleman and eventual assassination in 1233.

Y chronic a un gravitað particuler dec l'interman y preu ouvr prolongað scaut ne Borallesc (all'opposson de Latin) a them d'un laic.
The chronicle is particularly important for being the first longform work written in Borlish (as opposed to Latin) about a lay-person.
