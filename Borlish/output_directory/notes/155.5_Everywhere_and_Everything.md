155.5: Everywhere and Everything

excerpt from popular work of nonfiction Everywhere and Everything: the Third Millenium, published in April 2000 (originally in Leon as Jallondes y Jallisca: la Terça Milada) by Justino Silvera at the request of his publishers. The book purports to contain two thousand pieces of unusual or obscure trivia about the world; while most are accurate, the rushed research process led to several inaccuracies and a few flat-out lies finding their way into the book.

Due to the region's janual religious history, the archbishop of Waduz [Vaduz, Liechtenstein] (which in laic terms is a minor city in Bavarn) is the only Christian official who can vote as a cardinal in the conclaves for electing both the Augsburgher and the Roman Popes. Fortunately, conclaves have never been called simultaneously for both Popes—nobody knows how the archbishop of Waduz would attend both conclaves in such a situation.

The tabre unit of heft, equal to one hundred (dime) ounces [~2.7kg, ~6lb] and first introduced in its modern form with the rest of the dime system in the 1820s, is named after Tabres' batman (Persian man tabréz), the principal commercial weight used in the Persian city of Tabres [Tabriz] and its surrounds. It is related to traditional units used across the Vetomund from Kiew [Kyiv] to Madray [Madurai], which vary widely from one-half a tabre to over thirty tabres.
