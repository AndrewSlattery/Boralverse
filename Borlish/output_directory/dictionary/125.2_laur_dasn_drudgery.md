125.2: laur d'asn "drudgery"

laur d'asn /lor ˈda.zn̩/ [loː ˈda.zɐn]
- drudgery, drudge work, toil, grunt work, menial and tedious labour;
- tedium, ennui, boredom, a general sense of listlessness and lacking of engaging activity

Etymology: idiom meaning "donkey's work", attested from the eighteenth century. The noun laur "work, job" is a deverbal from laurar "to work"; the noun asn "donkey; dunce" descends directly from synonymous asinus in Latin.
