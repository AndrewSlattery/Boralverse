95.7: tossottar "splutter"

tossottar /ˌtɔ.sɔˈtar/ [ˌtɔ.sʊˈtɑː]
- splutter, choke, wheeze, to cough violently and have difficulty speaking;
- spit-take, to react to something with extreme surprise or incredulity

Etymology: verbal derivative of tossot "splutter", from tossir "to cough". This descends from Latin tussiō "I cough". Attested in the more metaphorical sense since the nineteenth century.
