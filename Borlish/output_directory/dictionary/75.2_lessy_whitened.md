75.2: lessy "whitened"

lessy /lɛˈsi/ [lɪˈsi]
- whitened, bleached, having been acted upon by a bleaching agent;
- faded, washed out, worn out, played out, having lost an original colour or more generally having lost vitality or energy;
- (of hair, chocolate etc) white, considered as a deviation from a former or more basal colour;
- bleach blond(e), having been treated so as to significantly lighten the colour (of hair)

Etymology: originally in the first sense from Old Boral leissi "bleached, whitened; (n.) lye soap". This descends from Vulgar Latin lixīvia "lye", via lixīvus "made of lye" from lixa "lye, alkaline water".
