69.2: contentar "appease"

contentar /ˌkɔn.tɛnˈtar/ [ˌkɔn.tɪnˈtɑː]
- satisfy, sate, to meet the needs of by doing what is required for someone;
- appease, placate, to make calm by adapting to the demands of someone;

also
se contentar /se ˌkɔn.tɛnˈtar/ [sɪˌkɔn.tɪnˈtɑː]
- content oneself, be satisfied, make do, to not want anything more than

Etymology: verbal derivation from adjective content "happy, glad, satisfied", which descends directly from Latin contentus "satisfied, lit. contained". This is the past participle of contineō "I keep, contain, fasten". Reflexive use is attested as early as the eleventh century, and the slightly extended senses relating to appeasement appear from the fifteenth.
