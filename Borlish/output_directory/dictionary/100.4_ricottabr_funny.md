100.4: ricottabr "funny"

ricottabr /ˌri.kɔˈta.br̩/ [ˌʀi.kʊˈta.bɐ]
- funny, humorous, hilarious, provoking laughter and mirth;
- witty, jocose, droll, waggish, showing ingenuity in an amusing way

Etymology: derivative via the -abr "-able" suffix of verb ricottar "laugh", itself a derivative of noun ricot "laugh, laughter". This is an idiosyncratic diminutive formation from Old Borlish verb rir "laugh" (now obsolete), from Late Latin rīdō "I laugh" (cf. synonymous rīdeō in Classical Latin).
