147.3: 11th Lexember - alcant aposc "eventually"

alcant aposc /alˈkant aˈpɔx/ [ɐˈgan.tɐˈpɔx]
- some day, in the future, eventually, at some point, after an unspecified amount of time;
- (interjection) about time, finally, jibe at someone or something taking a notionally excessive amount of time

Etymology: idiom found in this form since the eighteenth century, literally "once later". The adverb alcant "once" is an analogical form derived from cant "when" with the word element alc- < Latin aliquī "some". The adverb aposc "later" is from the preposition posc "after", from Latin post quid "after what". As an interjection it is only securely attested in the twentieth century.
