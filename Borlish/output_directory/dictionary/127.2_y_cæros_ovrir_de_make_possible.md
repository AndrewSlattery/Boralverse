127.2: y cæros ovrir de "make possible"

y cæros ovrir de /i keˈrɔz ɔˈvrɪr de/ [i kɪˈʀɔz ʊˈvʀɪː de]
- enable, make possible, allow for, permit, to create conditions such that (something) is more feasible or more likely to occur

Etymology: idiom from the nineteenth century, literally "to open the opportune moment of". The verb ovrir descends (under the influence of covrir "cover") via Old Borlish ovrir, avrir from Latin aperiō "I reveal, open". The noun cæros "window of opportunity, opportune moment" is a modern borrowing from Ancient Greek καιρός "period of time, season; opportunity".
