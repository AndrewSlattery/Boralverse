81.3: flaut "boating"

flaut /flot/ [flot]

    boating, sailing, the activity of going out onto water in a vessel;
    fleet, armada, a collection of sea-going or river-going vessels

Etymology: direct descendant from Old Boral flaut "fleet, group of ships", a borrowing from Old English flēot "ship, group of ships" (related to float).

    Il faun flaut casc juglot.
    /ɪl fon flot kax ʒajˈlɔt/
    [ɪl fon flot kax ʝɐjˈlɔt]
    3p do-3p boating each July
    They go boating each July.
