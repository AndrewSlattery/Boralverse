202.4: mornç a morn "piece of the action"

mornç a morn /ˈmɔr.n̩ts a mɔrn/ [ˈmɔːns a mɔːn]
- piece of the action, foot in the door, an opportunity to take part in some activity;
- percentage, cut, commission, share, a portion of the monetary (or otherwise) gains produced by some venture

Etymology: idiom from the early modern period, literally meaning "bite at a carrot". The phrase was coined by the poet Bautrmeu as the title of her 1654 compendium of works. The word mornç "bite" is a noun from verb mordr "bite", which descends from Latin mordeō "I bite"; the word morn "carrot" was borrowed in the Old Borlish period from Old English moran, plural of more "carrot, parsnip".
