199.6: Lexember 2025 - 13: tannir "set"

tannir /taˈnɪr/ [tɐˈnɪː]
- tan, to change animal hide into leather through soaking in tannic acid;
- fire, bake, kiln, to treat (e.g. pottery) with heat;
- cure, pickle, preserve, to alter (e.g. meat) by chemical treat for preservation or use;
- set, fix, mordant, to make (e.g. dye) fast or permanent;
- vulcanise, to treat (rubber) with sulfur to make it harder and more durable

Etymology: Middle Borlish, verbal derviation from the noun tan "oak bark" (from which tannic acid is obtained). This is a borrowing from synonymous tan in Old French, ultimately from Gaulish tannos "green oak". Extension to various other procedure for making materials harder or more durable is seen through the early modern period and into the twentieth century.
