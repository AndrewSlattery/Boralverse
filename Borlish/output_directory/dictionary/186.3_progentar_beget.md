186.3: progentar "beget"

progentar /ˌpro.ʒɛnˈtar/ [ˌpʀo.ʝɪnˈtɐː]
- (archaic) beget, sire, father, mother, to be the parent of;
- originate, give rise to, create, invent, devise, to cause to come into existence;
- inspire, provoke, engender, to cause to occur or to be felt;

Etymology: the verb is an early modern backformation of older progentour "creator", a borrowing from Latin prōgenitor "ancestor". This is an agent noun derivation from verb prōgignō "I beget, produce". In metaphorical use for causing actions or feelings the word is seen from the early nineteenth century.
