99.2: vrogs "waist"

vrogs /vrɔjz/ [vʀɔjz]
- waist, the part of the body between the pelvis and the stomach;
- midriff, midsection, abdomen, the middle section of the torso below the chest;
- waistline, (the length of) a line around the body or an article of clothing at waist height;
- neck, pinch, a middle section of something that is narrower than on either side

Etymology: from Old Borlish vroȝse, vorȝse, voȝrse "waist; belt, girdle", showing extensive metathetic variation. This is thought to be borrowed from a Borland Kelt form vocris "belt" related to Classical Kelt crissus "shirt".
