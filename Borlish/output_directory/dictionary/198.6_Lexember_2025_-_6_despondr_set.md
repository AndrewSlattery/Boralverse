198.6: Lexember 2025 - 6: despondr "set"

despondr /dɛˈspɔn.dr̩/ [dɪˈspɔn.dɐ]
- arrange, organise, set up, to put in useful positions;
- prepare, lay, set, to make ready for use by placing things on or around it in a particular arrangement;
- incline, mind, dispose, prime, to make (someone) more likely to act in a particular fashion;
- have access to, have available, avail oneself, have on hand, to be able to conveniently use;
- employ, have employed, make use of, to have access to the labour or benefits of

Etymology: descending from Old Borlish desponre "distribute, set out" or reborrowed during the Middle Borlish period from its source in Latin dispōnō "I arrange". The latter senses, likely influenced by cognates in other Romance languages, are intransitive and used with preposition de "of, from".
