59.3: doujug grader "hierarchy"

doujug grader /duˈʒaj graˈdɛr/ [dʊˈʝaj gʀɐˈdɛː]
- hierarchy, power structure, pecking order, ladder, an organisation of people or sometimes objects or concepts by rank

Etymology: idiom seen from the fifteenth century, literally "web of ranks". The noun doujug "cobweb; network, system" was originally a northern dialect term from the phrase doun jug /dundʒəj/ "July's down feathers", while the adjective grader is a regular derivation from grad "rank, (noble) title, status", from Latin gradus "step, rank".
