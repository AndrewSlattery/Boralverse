64.2: motivar "justify"

motivar /ˌmo.tiˈvar/ [ˌmo.tɪˈvɑː]
- justify, explain, excuse, rationalise, warrant, account for, to provide an acceptable explanation for;
- explicate, contextualise, decipher, elucidate, to make something make (more) sense;
- plan, map out, outline, arrange, to analyse a situation so that it becomes easier to understand

Etymology: Middle Boral, verbal derivation from motif "plan, project, grounds, reason, excuse". This descends from Late Latin mōtīvum "motive, cause", neuter of adjective mōtīvus "moved, stirred".
