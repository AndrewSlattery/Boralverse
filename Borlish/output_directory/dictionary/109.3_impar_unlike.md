109.3: impar "unlike"

impar /ɪmˈpar/ [ɪmˈpɐː]
- unlike, different from, not in a similar manner to;
- in contrast with, as opposed to, considered as differing from;
- disagreeing with, contradicting, not according to

Etymology: Middle Borlish legalese borrowing from Latin adjective impār "unequal", and used as a preposition almost as quickly by analogy with par "by, through, (archaic) according to" < Latin per "through".
