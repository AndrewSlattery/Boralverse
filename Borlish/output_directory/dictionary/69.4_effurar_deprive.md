69.4: effurar "deprive"

effurar /ˌɛ.fiˈrar/ [ˌɛ.fɪˈʀɑː]
- deprive, deny, dispossess, rob, to prevent from having or to cause to no longer have;
- keep out, keep away, repel, block, to deny access;

also s'effurar
- abstain, stay away, to choose not to partake

Etymology: Middle Boral borrowing from contemporary legal Latin effūror "I rob, I dispossess", from fūror "I steal, I plunder", a deponent verbal derivative of noun fūr "thief".
