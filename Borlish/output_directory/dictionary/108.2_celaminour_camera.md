108.2: celaminour "camera"

celaminour /tseˌla.miˈnur/ [tsɪˌla.mɪˈnʊː]
- camera, a device for taking still images (or later, also video);

also informally celam /tseˈlam/ [tsɪˈlam]

Etymology: nineteenth century, agentive derivative of Scholastic Latin celāminō "I create an image, I depict". This is related to Classical cælāmen "bas-relief, engraving". The abbreviation is attested from as early as 1891.
