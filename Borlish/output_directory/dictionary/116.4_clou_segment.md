116.4: clou "segment"

clou /klu/ [klu]
- clove, segment, one of the parts (as of a fruit or bulb) into which any body naturally separates or is divided;
- sector, piece, slice, part of a circle bounded by two radii and an arc they subtend (also of any circular object);
- faction, party, bloc, a political organisation of voters and/or politicians who share common goals

Etymology: from Old Borlish clou /klu(v)/ "clove (of garlic)", borrowed from synonymous clufu in Old English. The meaning was extended to other portions of things in the Middle Borlish period; in the phrase clou politic "political party" it is attested from the seventeenth century.
