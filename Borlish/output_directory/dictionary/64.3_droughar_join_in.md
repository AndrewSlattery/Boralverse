64.3: droughar "join in"

droughar /druˈjar/ [dʀʊˈʝɑː]
- join in, participate, take part, to involve oneself in some undertaking;
- help out, contribute, to provide assistance;
- interfere, intervene, step in, to become implicated in a situation

Etymology: from Old Boral drouȝar "to perform a service, to provide help, to carry out an errand", borrowed from Old English drēogan "to do (a duty), to endure, to bear". At first especially with religious connotations, as of providing Christian charity.
