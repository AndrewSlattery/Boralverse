59.4: validar "certify"

validar /ˌva.liˈdar/ [ˌva.lɪˈdɑː]
- certify, validate, qualify, to provide with a certification of mastery in some skill or to attest to the correctness or something;
- testify, corroborate, substantiate, back up, to confirm or support a statement with additional evidence;
- test, prove, verify, authenticate, to check the validity of worthiness of

Etymology: from Middle Boral validar, a borrowing from Scholastic Latin validō "I render (something) worthy or valid". This is a non-Classical derivative from adjective validus "strong, healthy, worthy, valid" originally used in legal settings.
