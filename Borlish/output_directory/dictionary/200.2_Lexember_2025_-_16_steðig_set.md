200.2: Lexember 2025 - 16: steðig "set"

steðig /steˈðaj/ [stɪˈðaj]
- obstinate, stubborn, resolute, convinced of one's rightness and resistant to correction;
- determined, bent, stuck, steadfast, firmly intending to stick to a particular course of action or point of view;
- set, (of a feeling or belief) fixed and not liable to change

Etymology: descendant of late Old Borlish steðiȝ "stable, constant", borrowed from an early Middle English or Middle Dutch derivative of a cognate of stead "place, position". Initially describing strong convictions, the word has been extended to describe people with such convictions since the seventeenth century.
