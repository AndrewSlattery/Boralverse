191.4: hoðic "reeve"

hoðic /hoˈðɪk/ [hʊˈðɪk]
- reeve, headman, official, village leader, a person with authority in a small community;
- chief, chieftain, the leader of a clan or tribe;
- councillor, alderman, a member of a local governmental body

Etymology: descendant of Old Borlish houðic, hoðinc "village leader; esp. leader of a Norse settlement in the north of Borland". This is a borrowing from Old Norse hǫfðingi "chief, lord", which is a derivation of hǫfuð "head". The term was first used for elected positions in town or city councils in the fifteenth century.
