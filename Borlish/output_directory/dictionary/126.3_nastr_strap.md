126.3: nastr "strap"

nastr /ˈnastr̩/ [ˈna.stɐ]
- strap, thong, a long narrow strip of leather or similar material;
- belt, band, a flat loop of material;
- whip, lash, a strip commonly of leather used to create a noise or to strike

Etymology: via Old Borlish nasle "strap, thong" and an identical Old French word from a Classical German noun derivative of nastiō "bind, tie, braid, plait". Extended to the tool used in animal husbandry and as a weapon in the Middle Borlish period.
