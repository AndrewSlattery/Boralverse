178.4: y voç se hoccovar "clear one's throat"

y voç se hoccovar /i vɔts se ˌhɔ.koˈvar/ [i vɔts se ˌhɔ.kʊˈvɐː]
- clear one's threat, harrumph, to cough in order to make it easier to speak or to attract people's attention to you

Etymology: idiom attested from the eighteenth century, literally "to cough the voice". The noun voç "voice" descends directly from synonymous vox, vocis in Latin. The verb hoccovar "cough" is a derivation from noun hocco "cough, ahem, hem-hem", thought to be of onomatopoeic origin.
