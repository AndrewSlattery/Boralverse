86.2: improbar "disapprove of"

improbar /ˌɪm.proˈbar/ [ˌɪm.pʀʊˈbɑː]
- disapprove of, condemn, blame, to think badly of someone for their actions;
- criticise, critique, find fault with, censure, to enumerate things one disapproves of;
- scold, rebuke, reprove, tell off, to speak harshly to due to disapproval of someone's behaviour

Etymology: backformation from improbation "criticism, critique, condemnation, censure, blame, disapproval" (originally in legal use for "official reprimand for immorality"), from synonymous improbātiō in Latin.
