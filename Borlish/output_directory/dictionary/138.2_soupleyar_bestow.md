138.2: soupleyar "bestow"

soupleyar /ˌsu.pliˈjar/ [ˌsu.plɪˈʝɐː]
- bestow, grant, impart, gift, confer, to present something to someone freely (especially as an honour or a sign of good favour);
- equip, render, supply, kit out with, to bring as provisions or equipment

Etymology: via Old Borlish sopleir "fill up, furnish, supply, provide" (later conflated with sopleȝar "require, take, consider necessary") from synonymous suppleō in Latin. Used for gifts originally in the context of granting land and titles to people.

Nos som plait de l'ambit agathiner vos soupleyar.
