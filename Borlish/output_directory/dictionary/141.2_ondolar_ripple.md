141.2: ondolar "ripple"

ondolar /ˌɔn.doˈlar/ [ˌɔn.dʊˈlɐː]
- ripple, undulate, billow, roil, eddy, to move like the disturbed surface of a body of water;
- roll, wave, to be shaped with undulations (as of hills or hair);
- bristle, flinch, recoil, to react with surprise or annoyance

Etymology: directly from Late Latin undulō "I rise in waves, undulate", a derivation from the noun unda "wave, billow". In metaphorical use it is attested form the Middle Borlish period; the emotional extension from the seventeenth century.
