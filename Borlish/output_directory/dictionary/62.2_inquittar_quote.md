62.2: inquittar "quote"

inquittar /ˌin.kwɪˈtar/ [ˌɪŋ.kwɪˈtɑː]
- quote, excerpt, cite, to repeat the exact words that a person has said or written;
- testify, attest, to bear witness to the veracity of something;

Etymology: seventeenth-century verbal derivative of inquit "quote, citation, excerpt, attestation". The noun inquit is a learned borrowing from Latin inquit "he/she said", a personal form of the highly defective verb inquam "I say".
