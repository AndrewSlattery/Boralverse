78.3: joivr "frost"

joivr /ˈʒɔj.vr̩/ [ˈʝɔj.vɐ]
- frost, rime, cover of ice crystals on objects that are exposed to the air analogous to dew;
- cold, cold weather, conditions below the freezing point of water when frost is liable to form;
- (telecommunications) noise, static, any part of a signal that reduces the fidelity of its transmission

Etymology: descendant of Old Boral givre, guivre "frost", borrowed from Old French and thought to originate in a Frankish form like gibara cognate with Willem gever "saliva".
