91.2: cambr pijon "lodgings"

cambr pijon /ˈkam.br̩ piˈʒɔn/ [ˈkam.bɐ pɪˈʝɔn]
- lodging house, boarding house, a building containing rooms for long-term rent and usually providing meals and cleaning;
- (a single) rented room in such a building

Etymology: collocation first used in the eighteenth century, from cambr "bedroom" and pijon "rent". The noun cambr "bedroom, quarters" descends directly from Latin camera "chamber, room"; the noun pijon "rent" descends from Latin pēnsiō "payment, pension".
