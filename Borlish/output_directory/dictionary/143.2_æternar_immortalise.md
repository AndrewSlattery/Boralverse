143.2: æternar "immortalise"

æternar /ˌe.tɛrˈnar/ [e.tɪːˈnɐː]
- perpetuate, continue, maintain, prolong, eternise, to make last indefinitely;
- immortalise, to cause to be renowned;
- memorialise, commemorate, mark, to ensure that someone or something is remembered

Etymology: early modern borrowing from Latin aeternō "I perpetuate, make renowned", a verbal derivative of aeternus "eternal, immortal" (compare much earlier adjective æternal "endless, eternal"). Made popular in the last senses after the Millstone War in the early twentieth century by its use in a Faðig war poem.
