152.4: bosaç "gunfire"

bosaç /boˈzats/ [bʊˈzas]
- gunfire, gunshot, report, the act or sound of shooting a gun;
- shooting, shootout, a confrontation in which guns are fired;
- criticism, disapproval, flak, censure, bad press, opprobrium, the bad opinion of others

Etymology: 20C, pejorative or augmentative derivation from bos "gun"; attested almost immediately in the metaphorical senses. The noun bos "gun" (cf. Merch boss "gun") is borrowed from Willem Dutch (arm)bósz "gun", which descends via armbaust "crossbow" and synonymous Old French arbaleste from Latin arcuballista "crossbow".
