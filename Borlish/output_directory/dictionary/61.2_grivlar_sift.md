61.2: grivlar "sift"

grivlar /griˈvlar/ [gʀɪˈvlɑː]
- sift, sieve, winnow, to sort (e.g. wheat from chaff) using a sieve or similar implement (also metaphorically);
- qualify, moderate, hedge, limit, restrict, to amend a statement of fact with some mitigating clause;
- sharpen, clarify, refine, to be more precise about a statement;
- sharpen, upscale, to render an image with greater resolution

Etymology: verbal derivation in the Old Boral period from grivle "sieve" (modern grivr). This noun descends from Vulgar Latin criblum, a dissimilated form of cribrum "sieve".
