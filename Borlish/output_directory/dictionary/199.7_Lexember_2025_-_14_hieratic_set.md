hieratic /ˌhje.raˈtɪk/ [ˌhje.ʀɐˈtɪk]
- priestly, sacerdotal, pertaining to priests or analogous religious figures;
- (specifically) hieratic, pertaining to pharaonic priests in Ancient Egypt or to their cursive system of writing;
- impassive, phlegmatic, unemotional, not having or revealing any sentiment;
- stern, set, severe, unyielding, (of expression or countenance) fixed or rigid without any leniency

Etymology: borrowed directly via Scholastic Latin from Greek ἱερατικός • hieratikós "priestly", from the root ῐ̔ερός • hĭerós "sacred". In extended use from the seventeenth century, in parallel with other languages like Vascon, presumably in reference to the stereotypical manner and bearing of priests.
