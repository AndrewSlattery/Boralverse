201.3: Lexember 2025 - 24: jagatteyar "set"

jagatteyar /ˌʒa.gaˈtjar/ [ˌʝa.gɐˈtjɐː]
- set, to place the action of (a fictional or imaginative work) in a particular setting;
- situate, contextualise, to provide surrounding details for;
- (intransitive) worldbuild, to develop a fictional setting

Etymology: early 20th century, verbal derivation from jagat "setting, world, continuity". This comes via Vascon from Gurjat જગત • jagat "world, universe", used as a term of art for "setting" in literary criticism. In the last sense the word enters general use only around the turn of the millennium.
