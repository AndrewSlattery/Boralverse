68.2: ne ðougl "rushed"

ne ðougl /ne ðujl/ [nɪˈðʊ.jʊ~nɪˈðwɪw]
- rushed, hasty, slapdash, done in an overly quick way due to a lack of time;

also fair ne ðougl knock off, dash off, to accompishly something hastily

Etymology: literally "in a flurry, in a gust of wind", idiom first attested in the fourteenth century. The noun ðougl "flurry, gust" reflects Old Boral ðouȝl, deverbal noun from ðoȝlar "waft, gust, float on the wind"; this verb descends from Latin adoleō "I emit an odour, I smell".
