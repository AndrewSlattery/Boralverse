82.4: scenar "stage"

scenar /xeˈnar/ [çɪˈnɑː]
- stage, put on, produce, perform, to put together a theatrical production as an acting troupe;
- orchestrate, undertake, arrange, direct, carry out, to plan and go through with some scheme or undertaking with many moving parts

Etymology: Middle Boral verbal derivation from scen "(theatre) stage; platform, soapbox" (formerly sometimes re-classicised as scæn), borrowed from Latin scæna "stage, scenery" from Ancient Greek σκηνή "tent, stage". In metaphorical use seen from the eighteenth century.
