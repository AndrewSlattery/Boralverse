189.3: lasdr "pathetic"

lasdr /ˈlaz.dr̩/ [ˈla.zdɐ]
- (archaic) leprous, infected with one of the diseases known as leprosy;
- pitiable, pitiful, pathetic, wretched, arousing feelings of sympathy or pathos;
- contemptible, despicable, deplorable, arousing feelings of scorn or disdain;
- ineffectual, impotent, occurring to little useful effect

Etymology: Middle Borlish borrowing from contemporary French lasdre "n. leper, wretch; adj. leprous, wretched". This descends from Late Latin Lazarus, in reference to a beggar in the New Testamement who was traditionally understood to have been a leper.
