175.2: nuivr "hilt"

nuivr /naivr̩/ [ˈnai.vɐ]
- hilt, haft, grip, the part of a sword or similar weapon that one holds in the hand;
- handle, the analogous part of any handheld tool;
- root, bitter end, quick, core, the furthest or most extreme portion or some thing or process

Etymology: aphaeretic form of earlier manuivre "hilt" attested from the fifteenth century, a direct descendant of synonymous manubrium in Latin. In metaphorical use it is most often seen in idioms like vars y nuivr "to the bitter end, unreservedly".
