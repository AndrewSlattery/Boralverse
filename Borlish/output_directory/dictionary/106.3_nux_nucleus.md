106.3: nux "nucleus"

nux /nɪks/ [nɪks]
- (atomic) nucleus, the central part of an atom with the great majority of its mass;
- (cell) nucleus, the organelle in a cell where genetic material is stored;
- core, hub, centre, the central part of something

Etymology: eighteenth century, borrowed from Scholastic Latin nux "nut; cell nucleus". Extended to atomic nuclei upon their discovery in 1902.
