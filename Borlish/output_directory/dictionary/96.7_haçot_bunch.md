96.7: haçot "bunch"

haçot /haˈtsɔt/ [hɐˈtsɔt]
- bunch, cluster, a group of similar things either growing together or found together in a clump;
- bundle, package, a group of objects held together by tying or wrapping;
- an unspecified but sizable number of things;
- lode, an unusual concentration of ore

Etymology: Middle Borlish diminutive of haç "bundle", borrowed in nautical use from synonymous haç in Vascon. This reflects Latin fascis "packet, package; burden, load".
