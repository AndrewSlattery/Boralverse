148.7: 22th Lexember - piver "livestock"

piver /piˈvɛr/ [pɪˈvɛː]
- livestock, cattle, farm animals, creatures domesticated for any type of cultivation;
- breeding, husbandry, ranching, the cultivation of domesticated animals;
- (work that is) practical, hands-on, experiential, first-hand experience as opposed to theoretical knowledge

Etymology: via Old Borlish peȝvair "livestock" from Latin pecuārius "pertaining to sheep or cattle", from pecū "domestic animals". In the last sense attested from the early modern period, especially in constructions like y piver de X "the practical skills required to do X".
