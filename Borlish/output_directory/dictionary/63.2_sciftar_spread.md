63.2: sciftar "spread"

sciftar /xɪfˈtar/ [çifˈtɑː]
- spread, smear, to distribute a thin layer of some material or subtance onto a surface;
- cover, layer, to arrange into layers;
- tidy up, neaten, sort out, cause to be in a more pleasing or less cumbersome arrangement;
- (metaphorically) tie in a bow, resolve, take care of, to deal with something in a way which is satisfying

especially quelt sciftar /kwɛlt xɪfˈtar/ make the bed, to arrange one's bedsheets neatly

Etymology: from Old Boral sciftar "to arrange, tidy", borrowed from Old English sciftan "divide, apportion, arrange". Specialisation to senses to do with layering is seen from the sixteenth century, likely by analogy with making the bed.
