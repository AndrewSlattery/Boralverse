155.2: maugr "margin"

maugr /ˈmojr̩/ [ˈmo.ʝɐ]
- margin, the edge of a flat surface (especially of paper or another material written upon);
- fringe, verge, a decorated edge;
- edge, border, flank, brink, the area near the edge of anything;
- outskirts, the region surrounding a built-up area;
- sidelines, the outside of any activity

Etymology: via Old Borlish marȝr, mauȝr "margin, edge" from Latin margō "border, margin, edge". In the geographical sense originaly referring specifically to the rural areas near to the city of Damvath. The term is seen in the last metaphorical sense from the eighteenth century onward.
