115.4: quarvol "airport"

quarvol /kwarˈvɔl/ [kwɐːˈvɔl]
- airport, an area designed for landing and takeoff of commercial passenger aircraft

Etymology: early twentieth century, portmanteau of quarvet "(bus, taxi, ferry) station, a stopping place for transportation" and vol "flight". The noun quarvet is a diminutive of quarf "docks, harbour", borrowed in the Old Borlish period from Old English hwearf "embankment, wharf". The noun vol is a deverbal from volar "fly", from Latin volō "I fly".
