53.3: mandrin "cyanide"

mandrin /manˈdrɪn/ [mɐnˈdʀɪn]
- cyanide, a compound containing a -C≡N group or the CN⁻ anion;
- (specifically) hydrogen cyanide, a colourless and very poisonous liquid;

(not to be confused with sandrin "vitamin C, ascorbic acid".)

Etymology: adjectival derivation from mandr "almond" (from the presence of cyanide in almonds) first used in expressions like mandrin a lix "potassium cyanide" in the eighteenth century.
