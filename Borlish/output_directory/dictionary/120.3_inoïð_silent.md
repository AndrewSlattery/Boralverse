120.3: inoïð "silent"

inoïð /ˌɪ.nɔjˈɪθ/ [ˌɪ.nʊˈjɪh]
- silent, soundless, noiseless, without any sound;
- unheard, unnoticed, ignored, forgotten, unrecognised, not acknowledged;
- unheard-of, unprecedented, never before having been done or otherwise experienced

Etymology: negated past participle of oïr "to hear", seen in usage extended from the literal "unheard" from the twelfth century. The verb descends directly from Latin audiō "I hear". In the specific sense "unprecedented" it is seen from the seventeenth century.
