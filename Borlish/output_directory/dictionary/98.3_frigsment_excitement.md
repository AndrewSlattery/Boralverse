98.3: frigsment "excitement"

frigsment /frajzˈmɛnt/ [fʀɐjzˈmɛnt]
- excitement, enthusiasm, thrill, the state of being excited;
- arousal, horniness, randiness, the state of feeling sexual lust;
- frenzy, fuss, tumult, commotion, clamour, ado, bustle, a general state of things or people being disturbed or riled up

Etymology: noun formed from frigsar "to fry; to excite, arouse, rile up". This reflects Late Latin frīxō "I fry", a secondary formation from Classical frīgō based on the perfect stem.
