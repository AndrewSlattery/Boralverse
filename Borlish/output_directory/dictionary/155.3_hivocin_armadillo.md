155.3: hivocin "armadillo"

hivocin /ˌhi.voˈtsɪn/ [ˌhi.vʊˈtsɪn]
- armadillo, any of several burrowing mammals covered with bony protective plates;
- (colloquial) recluse, loner, hermit, wallflower, shrinking violet, a shy or retiring person whom it is hard to get to know

Etymology: from Vascon hivocin or Scholastic Latin hivocīnus, each having come via Morrove Arabic from a Mayattan language, plausibly Gish 'ibach "armadillo". In the metaphorical sense the word is attested from the early twentieth century, after the nickname of a character in popular children's book Figl a Fojon "Children of the Quarry".
