96.2: gehennar "embarrass"

gehennar /ˌʒe.hɛˈnar/ [ˌʝe.hɪˈnɑː]
- embarrass, humiliate, abash, discomfit, to disrupt someone's comfort;
- confuse, perplex, disconcert, bewilder, to bring into a state of confusion;
- inconvenience, discommode, to hinder the plans of

Etymology: borrowed along with noun gehen "embarrassment" from Middle French géhenne "humiliation, punishment". This reflects Old French gehine "reprisal" and gehir "to confess" (both from Frankish), respelt under the influence of unrelated Géhenne "Gehenna, Hell".
