169.3: compeðour "candidate"

compeðour /ˌkɔm.peˈður/ [ˌkɔm.pɪˈðʊː]
- competitor, contender, a person or team taking part in a competition;
- rival, challenger, a person or team against whom one is competing;
- candidate, an individual or party seeking election to a position;
- option, alternative, one of the candidates for (originally) an elected position or (colloquially) any responsibility or requirement

Etymology: Middle Borlish, agent derivation from the verb compeðir "compete, contend, fight". This descends from Latin competō "I am equal to, I am capable of". The word has been used in political contexts since the late seventeenth century, although it only became widespread a hundred years later.
