110.3: massour "jaw"

massour /maˈsur/ [mɐˈsʊː]
- jaw, jowl, the part of the face below the mouth;
- jawbone, mandible, the bone of the lower jaw;
- vice, clamp, tongs, grip, any tool comprising two parts which close together to hold something in place

Etymology: Middle Borlish masçour "jaw(bone)" respelt in the 1870 reform, agentive derivation from masçar "chew, bite down on". This straightforwardly reflects post-classical Latin masticō "I chew", from Greek. In non-anatomical use the word is attested from the sixteenth century.
