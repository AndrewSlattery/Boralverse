124.4: dignant "appropriate"

dignant /dajˈnant/ [dɐjˈnant]
- becoming, suitable, appropriate, fit, done, conforming to standards of behaviour or aesthetics;
- named, called, known as, AKA, alias, having been given an appellation

Etymology: present participle of dignar "merit, deserve, be worthy of", itself a verbal derivation from Old Borlish diȝn "worth, merit". This reflects Latin adjective dignus "worthy, appropriate, meet". The latter senses are seen from the late Middle Borlish period.
