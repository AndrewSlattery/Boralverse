145.2: oustar "smite"

oustar /uˈstar/ [ʊˈstɐː]
- (formal, historical) slay, kill, to end the life of;
- smite, to strike down in the manner of divine punishment;
- stupefy, stun, amaze, to leave incapacitated due to shock;
- charm, delight, enchant, to engender feelings of extreme happiness or infatuation in

Etymology: descendant via Old Borlish oustar "kill, slay; thwart, stop, defeat" from Latin obstō "oppose, block, hinder". Its more violent senses survive mostly through their use in early translations of the Bible into Borlish; the latter senses are more common in the modern language.
