21.3: yert "awake"

yert /jɛrt/ [ʝɛːt]
adj.
- awake, up, not sleeping and conscious of the world;
- alert, vigilant, capable of reacting quickly to new information and especialy to danger;
adv.
- vertically, upwards, downwards, following a trajectory that is roughly straight up or down

Etymology: from Old Boral yert(e) "standing, upright" (cf. Mostara yerto "stiff, rigid" or Lombard erto "steep"), a regular descendant of Vulgar Latin ērctus "upright, alert". This is a variant of synonymous ērēctus, the past participle of ērigō "I raise, build, erect".
