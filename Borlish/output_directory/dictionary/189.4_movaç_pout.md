189.4: movaç "pout"

movaç /moˈvats/ [mʊˈvats]
- pout, moue, an expression of displeasure via protruding lips;
- pet, brood, sulk, huff, mood, a fit of sullenness as characterised by pouting

Etymology: eighteenth century, pejorative noun formation from movar "pout, sulk". This reflects earlier moar, a borrowing from Old French verb moer and noun moe "grimace", each from a Frankish form reflecting Classical German mauvō "sleeve; protruding lip".
