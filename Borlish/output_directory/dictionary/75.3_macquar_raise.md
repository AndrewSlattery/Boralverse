75.3: macquar "raise"

macquar /maˈkwar/ [mɐˈkwɑː]
- raise, rear, bring up, to care for (a child) in the first years of their life;
- nurture, cultivate, grow, to garden or farm (plants);
- foster, educate, instruct, to act as a teacher for

Etymology: from Old Boral ma(c)quar "to raise (a child)", from synonymous Insular Latin maquō, maccō, likely of Kelt origin. If so, cognate with Irish mac "son" and Welsh magωr "cowherd" (formerly "breeder of animals").
