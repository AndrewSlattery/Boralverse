120.4: frastr "ash"

frastr /ˈfra.str̩/ [ˈfra.stɐ]
- ash tree, a deciduous tree in the olive family;
- ashwood, the wood of the ash tree;
- (historical) javelin, a light spear thrown as a lang-distance weapon;
- tough, resilient, sturdy, able to withstand considerable force;
- (slang) jacked, built, shredded, strapping, burly, notably muscular

Etymology: direct descendant via Old Borlish fra(i)sre "ash tree, ash wood, spear" from synonymous frāxinus in Latin. Ash wood has been used for spear shafts since pre-Classical times. As an adjective the word is seen from the fourteenth century; in its specific slang sense it is likely at most fifty years old.
