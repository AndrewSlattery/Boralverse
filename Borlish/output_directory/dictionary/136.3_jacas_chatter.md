136.3: jacas "chatter"

jacas /ʒaˈkaz/ [ʝɐˈkaz]
- chatter, jabber, prattle, babble, inconsequential or childish talk;
- gossip, dirt, hearsay, scuttlebutt, idle talk about someone else's private affairs

Etymology: deverbal from jacassar "chatter; gossip", a borrowing from synonymous jacasser in French. This verb comes from jacasse "magpie; busybody", a derivative of the name Jacques. Possibly reinforced by the Merch jackass "male donkey".
