152.3: rustr "rural"

rustr /ˈrɪstr̩/ [ˈʀɪ.stɐ]
- rural, agrarian, rustic, of the countryside away from urban areas;
- provincial, backwoods, boorish, unsophisticated, unrefined, narrowminded, having a worldview considered typical of rural people;
- uncouth, awkward, clumsy, bumbling, unaware of niceties or unable to competently observe them

Etymology: from Old Borlish rustre "rural", either directly from Latin rūsticus "rural" or via synonymous rustre in Old French. The insulting senses date as far back as Classical times, and may have been reintroduced during the Middle Borlish period.
