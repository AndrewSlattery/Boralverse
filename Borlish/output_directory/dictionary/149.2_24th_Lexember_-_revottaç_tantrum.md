149.2: 24th Lexember - revottaç "tantrum"

revottaç /ˌre.vɔˈtats/ [ˌʀe.vʊˈtats]
- tantrum, fit, outburst, pique, apoplexy, a contemptible display of bad temper;
- scene, disruption, embarrassment, the result of somebody having a tantrum

Etymology: pejorative derivation from revot "annoyance, irritation". This is an alteration of earlier raivot, diminutive of now-obsolete raif "rage", which descends from Late Latin rabia "rage" (the usual modern word is voudy, a borrowing). The word is considered impolite as a description of an adult's behaviour.
