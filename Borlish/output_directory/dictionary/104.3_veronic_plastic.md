104.3: veronic "plastic"

veronic /ˌve.roˈnɪk/ [ˌve.ʀʊˈnɪk]
- plastic, a synthetic solid hydrocarbon-based polymer;
- (historical, in particular) celluloid, any of a variety of thermoplastics created from nitrocellulose and camphor used as photographic film;
- spinelessness, passivity, obligingness, the willing to comply or change oneself without objection;
adj.
- plastic, made of plastic;
- spineless, passive, obliging, willing to comply or change oneself without objection

Etymology: 19C, former trademark also written as lacq Veronic "Veronic shellac". The word was borrowed from Medieval Latin veronīx "resin, varnish", perhaps from Byzantine Greek Βερενῑ́κη (Bereníkē), a city of Cyrenaica. In metaphorical use the word is seen from as early as the 1920s.
