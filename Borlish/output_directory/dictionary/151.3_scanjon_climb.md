151.3: scanjon "climb"

scanjon /xanˈʒɔn/ [xɐnˈʝɔn]
- rise, ascent, climb, an increase in elevation;
- upgrade, uplift, overhaul, improvement, the replacement or betterment of something to have new or renewed features;
- boom, bonanza, a period of wealth or prestige

Etymology: noun from scandr "rise, climb" or directly from Latin scansiō "a climbing", from Latin scandō "I climb". The word is attested in metaphorical uses from the sixteenth century, and in its specifically economic sense it is seen from the nineteenth century.
