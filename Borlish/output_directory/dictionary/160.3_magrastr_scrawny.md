160.3: magrastr "scrawny"

magrastr /mɛiˈrastr̩/ [mɪjˈʀa.stɐ]
- scrawny, lean, gangly, lanky, weedy, being notably unmuscular;
- malnourished, gaunt, underweight, unheathily thin as from illness or hunger;
- flimsy, spindly, rickety, not held together securely and liable to break or fall apart;
- unsound, tenuous, sketchy, (of an idea) not convincing due to a lack of solid argumentation

Etymology: early Modern formation using the pejorative suffix -astr < Latin -aster "incomplete resemblance to" from adjective magr "limited, scarce, meagre". This descends directly via Old Borlish maȝre /ˈmaɣ.rə/ from Latin macer "lean, skinny". The word is attested in metaphorical use from the nineteenth century.
