83.4: linaç "lineage"

linaç /liˈnats/ [lɪˈnas]
- lineage, line, descent, a line or tree of offspring from a common ancestor;
- family, house, dynasty, a group of people related by descent (particularly when of noble birth);
- birth, parentage, blood, ancestry, one's characteristics as considered to be a result of the qualities of one's forebears

Etymology: from Old Boral linaç "lineage, parentage", probably borrowed from Old French but in any case related to lign "line; line of descent". In the sense "house, dynasty", attested from the twelfth century.
