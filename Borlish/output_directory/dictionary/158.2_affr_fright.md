158.2: affr "fright"

affr /ˈafr̩/ [ˈa.fɐ]
- fright, alarm, shock, terror, a sudden and violent feeling of fear;
- threat, nightmare, ordeal, a scary experience;
- monster, menace, something or someone who is scary;

Etymology: borrowed into Middle Borlish from French affre "horror, terror". This descends via Old Vascon affre from a Gothic root reflecting Classical German æbras "bitter, violent". The term has been used also for the emotion's cause since the eighteenth century.
