38.2: teir "to number"

teir /tir/ [tɪː]
- number, be, to consist of some number of people or things in a group;
- come to, cost, be worth some amount of money;
- be (at a distance of), be located, to exist at some specified distance from or height above a reference point;
- (obsolete except in certain idiomatic expressions) be had by, to be in the possession of somebody

Etymology: from Old Boral teir "to span, to cover (an amount of land), to stretch (a distance)", from Latin tegō "I cover up, I clothe". Extended to many contexts in which the object of a copular phrase is a quantity or amount of substance.
