105.3: ðonc "crooked"

ðonc /ðɔnk/ [ðɔŋk]
- crooked, bent, having one or more bends or angles;
- askew, tilted, twisted, off-centre, turned or positioned to one side;
- wry, ironic, sardonic, drily humorous or bitter;
- unlucky, unfortunate, beset by misfortune

Etymology: via Old Borlish from Latin aduncus "hooked, bent, curved", from uncus "hook" (whence Borlish onc "hook, clasp, link, trigger"). Metaphorical extension is seen from the fifteenth century.
