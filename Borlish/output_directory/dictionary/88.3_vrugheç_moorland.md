88.3: vrugheç "moorland"

vrugheç /vrajˈets/ [vʀɐˈʝɛs]
- heath, heathland, moor, moorland, a tract of level uncultivated land with sandy soil and scrubby vegetation;
- waste, wasteland, badlands, a place with no remaining resources or metaphorically any uninteresting place

Etymology: descendant of Old Boral vruȝeç "heath, moor", which is a nominal derivation of vruȝ "heather" (modern vrug) with the suffix -eç < Latin -itia. The word vruȝ is borrowed from Borland Kelt vrūg "heather", cognate with synonymous frágh in Irish.
