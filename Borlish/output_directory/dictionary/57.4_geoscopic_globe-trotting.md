57.4: geoscopic "globe-trotting"

geoscopic /ˌʒaw.xoˈpɪk/ [ˌʝaw.xʊˈpɪk]
- globe-trotting, worldwide, travelling all over the world;
- exhaustive, comprehensive, having a very wide scope;
- exploratory, blue-sky, engaging in research or investigation primarily for its own sake;
- (historical) pertaining to the maritime explorations of the Intertwining Century following the Novomundine Landfall

Etymology: early modern formation as if from Ancient Greek γεωσκοπῐκός "world-beholding", originally in the last sense. Metaphorical extension of the term is seen from the nineteenth century.
