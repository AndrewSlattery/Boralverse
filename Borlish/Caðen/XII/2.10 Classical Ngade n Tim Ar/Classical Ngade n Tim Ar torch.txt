CT has pretty rigid VOS word order. Aspectual particles precede verbs; derivational particles precede nouns and verbs. Adjectives follow the nouns they modify. The adpositions are inpositionsâ€”that is, they directly follow the noun but come between it and any modifiers, if present.

Plural nouns generally form by metathesis (initial consonants and immediately-following vowels swap places; e.g. #bac > #abc) or the use of a particle ar (for vowel-initial words).

The difference between the continuous and discontinuous perfective is the application, or lack thereof, of the act on a currently-relevant situation.

There's a lot of zero-derivation from verbs to nouns (usually producing resultatives).

Relative clauses get slotted into the appropriate spot in the sentence as a full sentence, with the relative pronoun/clausal subordinator hae used in the subclause in place of the relative referent.
