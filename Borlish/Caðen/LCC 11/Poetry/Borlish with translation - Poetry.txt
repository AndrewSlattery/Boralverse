Sperið apart son y derran sagaç,
Oç dithyramb es delivrað a Deu.
Jaçol jayen il rad ag joun kiveu
Sur poesy divin ja nou lengaç.

Par caum e kyriel a nossy cot
Gimmolm nos a gið divin gaddar.
Nuton parmainn ec noit a souquondar
Cauçant ag gur d'ivan ne sy senglot.

Ða stou y noit ðocar, nos ður tijon
Vancheu victoir den, vardau laure
Ke tienn toð y toun saivenç igne
Ðolent de veyaçour a tal fujon.

Sey allaçað y faur a Deu n'oc cont;
Y sculd a s'aïðar es paramont.

---

They are gone away, the last wise people,
An impassioned oration is delivered to God.
Once they gave advice to the young flock
Through divine poetry in an invented tongue.

By hymn and prayer in our cottages
We strive to truly understand divine guidance.
Will-o'-the-wisps still lurk below at night
Hunting for childish dread with its sobs.

If we must harrow the night, light firebrands for us
Let us catch victory with them, let us secure a triumph
For all the town holds onto blazing wisdom
Billowing in from angels in such abundance.

Let God's wisdom be understood in this tale;
The duty to help one another is supreme.

---
