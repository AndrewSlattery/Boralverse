[b]I n’a jamay l’ig disnary patronizað, pu lonc y nom i stima s’eç repast fort car.


Tant i bondisce y noit, i vu eç fem nell’accostur luxuir, hom a braç; il sorteurn de lour mobil e hastaurn n’ig ant magn solcið, curtel tot rimbandað. Avant, i ci passa tojorn por poðir y test tornar e ci spiar, tant i bondisce ni rout meðes y jorn (pu cos particulair y noit tart ni ceuðað). I sevie a bondir, deskeðant eð antecipant tot com lou.


Oy, portant, l’a tendanç a ses en tornar, a triar y ci rout descendr angostessem e foscessem, en afaç partient cos braf. Ig cas l’es ennoyað d’eç pas kieð e scadrið, e d’eç aðombraç silent e vagant. Parvig l’oy un ricot kieð acculant, eð i frigçon com fouil.[/b]


Boral is a romlang spoken on a fiction island in the North Sea, about twice the size of Wales.


Nouns
Nouns in Boral do not take any inflection, neither for number nor for case.


Determiners
These directly precede the word they modify. The four most important determiners are [b]oc[/b], [b]eç[/b], [b]ig[/b] and [b]ci[/b]. Combined with the definite article they form:
- [b]l’oc[/b] this
- [b]l’eç[/b] these
- [b]l’ig[/b] that
- [b]y ci[/b] those


Used alone, [b]oc eç ig ci[/b] can simply mark number (the details are more complicated but not relevant).


A further use of these words is as preverbal objects, as follows:
[b]Jo spy l’eç hom.[/b] “I look at these men.”
[b]J’eç spy.[/b] “I look at them/these.”


Verbs
Verbs are suffixing, marking for person and tense/aspect.
The three declensions of verbs which appear conjugated in this text are [b]-ar[/b], [b]-ir1[/b] and [b]-ir2[/b] verbs. The relevant endings are respectively:
- infinitive : [b]-ar[/b], [b]-ir[/b], [b]-ir[/b]
- 3s present indicative : [b]-∅[/b], [b]-isc[/b], [b]-∅[/b]
- 3s past imperfective : [b]-a[/b], [b]-isce[/b], [b]-ie[/b]
- 3s past perfective : [b]-au[/b], [b]-eu[/b], [b]-eu[/b]
- 3p past perfective : [b]-aurn[/b], [b]-eurn[/b], [b]-eurn[/b]
- active participle : [b]-ant[/b], [b]-iscent[/b], [b]-ient[/b]
- passive participle : [b]-að[/b], [b]-ið[/b], [b]-eð[/b]


Syntax


Main clauses in Boral are usually SVO (although see the preverbal object particles in Determiners). However, in subordinate clauses, the direct objects of participles usually precede the participle.
Adjectives in Boral follow the noun. Boral has prepositions which precede their argument.
