Cordin Editions (Borlish **Edition Cordin** /ˌe.diˈdzjɔn kɔrˈdɪn/) is a publishing house founded in the mid-twentieth century and based in the city of [[Cordin]] on [[Borland]].

The company, which is affiliated with the [[University of Cordin]], is recognised for producing educational books for national schools. It also published syllabi covering schooling throughout childhood which were taken up as a standard by most schools in the polity.

## Publications
- The Boral Province in the century after Hadrian (**Y Provinç Boral par y siecr posc Hadrian**, 1952), written by late classical historian [[Robiaut Scarvon]]
- The Story of Borland (**Y Stoir d'Istr Boral**, first edition 1982, second edition 1993), a school history textbook
- Shepherds of Hambrick: the Small History of Dane Borland (**Acr a Sauð Vellig**, 2008), written by [[Sconet Ydreç]] about Borland in the tenth century
- From Tablets to Touchscreens: The Story of Education (**Des Delt vars Dois: Y Stoir d'Education**, 2016), written by [[Theign Roufler]] in the style of an academic textbook but intended to be read by a large lay audience
