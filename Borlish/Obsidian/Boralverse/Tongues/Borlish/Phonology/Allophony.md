Throughout this section, the phonetic transcription approximately reflects the mesolectal speech in the capital city of Damvath.
### Assimilation
The phoneme /n/ is pronounced [ŋ] before velars.
‹blanc viking mensc›
/blank viˈkɪng mɛnx/
[blaŋk vɪˈkɪŋg mɛŋx]
'white raid honour'

Regressive voicing assimilation affects /s/ before any voiced obstruent, nasal or /l/.
‹sboc svanið pasnagl desjeun bruslaç›
/sbɔk svaˈnɪθ paˈsnɛjl dɛsˈʒaun briˈslats/
[zbɔk zvɐˈnɪθ pɐˈznɛjl dɪzˈʝaun bʀɪˈzlats]
'smirk unconscious parsnip breakfast sunburn'
### Palatals
The phoneme /x/ surfaces as [ç] when adjacent to a front vowel.
‹scið esc›
/xɪθ ɛx/
[çɪθ ɛç]
'possible bait'

The phoneme /ʒ/, along with /j/ either word-initially or between an unstressed and a stressed vowel (in that order), is pronounced [ʝ].
‹jast yon çuyal›
/ʒast jɔn tsaˈjal/
[ʝast ʝɔn tsɐˈʝal]
'zinc where cicada'
### Non-rhoticity
Syllabic /r̩/ surfaces as [ɐ]. Furthermore, for some speakers syllabic /m n/ surface as [ɐm ɐn] respectively.
‹issambr ðesmbal visn›
/ɪˈsamb.r̩ ˌðɛz.m̩ˈbal vɪz.n̩/
[ɪˈsam.bɐ ˌðɛ.zɐmˈbal ˈvɪ.zɐn]
'massacre dodgeball knot'

Coda /r/ surfaces as length on a preceding monophthong, or as [ɐ] after a diphthong or /j/. The vowel's quality may also be altered, with /ar ir ur/ becoming [ɐː ɪː ʊː] respectively.
‹vert hagr tourm›
/vɛrt ˈhɛj.r̩ turm/
[vɛːt ˈhɛ.jɐ tʊːm]
'green linchpin squadron'

When a word beginning with a vowel immediately follows, the [ʀ] is resurrected as the onset of the next syllable.
‹l'hour uncos›
/lur ɪnˈkɔz/
[lʊː‿ ʀɪŋˈkɔz]
'df=time something'
### Reduction
In syllables without primary or secondary stress, the vowel /a/ surfaces as [ɐ], the vowels /ɛ e i/ as [ɪ] and the vowels /ɔ o u/ as [ʊ].
‹tovaresc taisson torvegl›
/ˌto.vaˈrɛx teˈsɔn tɔrˈvɪjl/
[ˌto.vɐˈʀɛç tɪˈsɔn tʊːˈvɪjl]
'homosexual badger whirlwind'

Before an obstruent or after /n/, a coda /ts/ is pronounced [s].
‹amaçgat fenç›
/ˌa.matsˈgat fɛnts/
[ˌa.mɐsˈgat fɛns]
'nursery split'

The sequence /kts/ is realised as [ks].
‹acceir facçon›
/akˈtsir fakˈtsɔn/
[ɐkˈsɪː fɐkˈsɔn]
'approach party'
