214.2: madrugoir "nightspot"

madrugoir /ˌma.driˈgɔir/ [ˌma.dʀɪˈgɔjɐ]
- nightclub, nightspot, club, a venue that is open late at night to provide entertainment (especially alcoholic drinks, music and dancing);
- nightlife, clubland, the collection of venues open at night in a given location or the mass of people who frequent them;
- night out, bender, an (especially raucous) instance of going to a nightclub

Etymology: early 20C, place derivation from verb madrugar "to stay up late at night, (strictly) to pull an all-nighter". This was borrowed from Portingale madrugar, the root also of that language's madrugada "the small hours, the period between midnight and dawn". These descend metathetically via Old Portingale  madurgar "to rise early" from Vulgar Larin mātūricō "I hurry, make haste; (literally) I ripen", itself a derivative of mātūrus "ripe". Seen in extended use from the 1940s.
