69.4: effurar "deprive"

effurar /ňĆ…õ.fiňąrar/ [ňĆ…õ.f…™ňą Ä…Ďňź]
- deprive, deny, dispossess, rob, to prevent from having or to cause to no longer have;
- keep out, keep away, repel, block, to deny access;

also s'effurar
- abstain, stay away, to choose not to partake

Etymology: Middle Boral borrowing from contemporary legal Latin effŇęror "I rob, I dispossess", from fŇęror "I steal, I plunder", a deponent verbal derivative of noun fŇęr "thief".

Stou joun sgarant effurar dy hal sdroy vabian closnautr.
