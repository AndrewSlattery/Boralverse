205.3: vrolliscour "crowdpleaser"

vrolliscour /ňĆvr…Ē.liňąxur/ [ňĆv Ä…Ē.l…™ňąx äňź]
- entertainer, performer, one who puts on a show for an audience;
- crowdpleaser, someone or something that is (reliably) entertaining for an audience

Etymology: nineteenth century, agentive derivation from verb vrollir "please, entertain, cheer, gladden". This itself is a backformation from noun vrollisc "entertainment, fun", borrowed in the early modern period from a Willem or Saxon Dutch adjective like vrolic, frolich "merry, cheerful". In the modern period, when referring to a person, the word often denotes someone who has an online audience.
