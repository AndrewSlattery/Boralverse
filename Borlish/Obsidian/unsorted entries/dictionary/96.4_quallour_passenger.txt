96.4: quallour "passenger"

quallour /kwaňąlur/ [kw…źňąl äňź]
- passenger, one who rides or travels in a vehicle but who is not the driver or a crewmember;
- hanger-on, tagalong, someone who accompanies without contributing much;
- side dish, a small serving of food meant to accompany a main course;
- epiphyte, a plant which grows on another for support but which does not obtain nutrients from it

Etymology: agentive formation from dialect verb quallar "to ride, travel by horse", which along with qual "horse" are nonstandard variants of covallar and coval in standard Borlish. These all descend from Vulgar Latin caballus "horse", from Gaulish. In latter usages clipped from compounds desc quallour "passenger meal" and plant quallour "passenger plant".

Y quallour egreye des y convey posc catr lau.
