169.4: scavlar "ape"

scavlar /xaňąvlar/ [x…źňąvl…źňź]
- mirror, reflect, to produce a reflection of;
- copy, replicate, duplicate, clone, to make another identical version of;
- mimic, ape, parrot, imitate, to act in the same way as;
- satirise, parody, caricature, to mock by means of imitation

Etymology: Middle Borlish, verbal derivation from scavre, scavle "reflection, mirror image" (exemplifying the confusion regarding final -re/-le which affected contemporary Borlish). This descends from Old Borlish scaure "mirror; reflection; actor", a borrowing from Old English sńčńďawere "observer, watchman; mirror; jester, buffoon" (cognate with Modern English "show-er").
