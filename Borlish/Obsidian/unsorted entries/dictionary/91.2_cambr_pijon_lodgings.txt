91.2: cambr pijon "lodgings"

cambr pijon /ňąkam.brŐ© piňą í…Ēn/ [ňąkam.b…ź p…™ňą Ě…Ēn]
- lodging house, boarding house, a building containing rooms for long-term rent and usually providing meals and cleaning;
- (a single) rented room in such a building

Etymology: collocation first used in the eighteenth century, from cambr "bedroom" and pijon "rent". The noun cambr "bedroom, quarters" descends directly from Latin camera "chamber, room"; the noun pijon "rent" descends from Latin pńďnsiŇć "payment, pension".
