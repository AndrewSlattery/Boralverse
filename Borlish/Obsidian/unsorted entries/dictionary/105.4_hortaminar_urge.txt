105.4: hortaminar "urge"

hortaminar /h…ĒrňĆta.miňąnar/ [h äňźňĆta.m…™ňąn…źňź]
- encourage, exhort, incite, urge, press, to earnestly advise that someone should do something;
- implore, beseech, entreat, supplicate, prevail upon, to earnestly plead for something

Etymology: from Scholastic Latin hortńĀminŇć "I exhort, urge", a non-classical verbal derivation of hortńĀmen "incitement, encouragement". This comes from deponent verb hortor "I encourage". Originally in legal use, but seen in general contexts since the seventeenth century.
