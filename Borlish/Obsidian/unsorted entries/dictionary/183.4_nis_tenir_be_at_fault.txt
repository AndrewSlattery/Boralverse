183.4: nis tenir "be at fault"

nis tenir /n…™z teňąn…™r/ [n…™z t…™ňąn…™ňź]
- be responsible, take responsibility, to be the ultimate cause of something;
- be at fault, be liable, take the blame, to have something bad attributed to one;
- more generally, to be the origin or driving force behind something

Etymology: idiom from the seventeenth century, originally seen in legal contexts, literally "to hold effort". The noun nis "effort, vigour; force; obligation; attention" comes from Old Boral nis, ne»Ěs, ni»Ěs et al. "effort, vigour", which descends uncomplicatedly from Latin nńęxus, nńęsus "strain, forward momentum". This is the participle of deponent verb nńętor "I lean on, advance, strive, contend".
