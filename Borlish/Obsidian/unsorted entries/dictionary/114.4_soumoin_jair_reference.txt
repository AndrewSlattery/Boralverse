114.4: soumoin jair "reference"

soumoin jair /suňąm…Ēin  íer/ [s äňąm…Ēin  Ěeňź]
- reference, cite, to quote the words of another with attribution;
- credit, shout out, acknowledge, to publicly mention someone especially in order to thank them

Etymology: idiom meaning "to throw reference" attested from the seventeenth century. The noun soumoin is a deverbal of soumonir "prompt, remind", which descends from Latin summoneŇć "I privately advise, give a hint". The verb jair comes from Latin iaciŇć "I throw, fling".
