Etymology: From Middle Boral ombisc "surrender, capitulation, giving up; resignation, act of leaving a post", deverbal from now-obsolete ombijar "surrender, capitulate, give up; resign, leave a post". This descends with a change of valency from Old Boral onbi»Ěar "subdue, quash, overcome, bring under control", itself borrowed from local Old English onbiegan "bend, subdue".

The verb phrase translates literally as "to release resignation, to let go of resignation". Compare var souvr "declare war" and ley souvr "uphold the law".
