49.4: 8th Lexember

ploghar "process"

ploghar /pl…Ēňąjar/ [pl äňą Ě…Ďňź]
- till, cultivate, plough, to prepare (ground) for the growing of crops;
- process, render, work on, to apply a given process to;
- alter, synthesise, convert, to cause to undergo a process so as to turn a material into something else

Etymology: from Old Boral plo»Ěar "to till, plough" and now-obsolete noun plou»Ě "plough", both originally in western dialects and borrowed from Old English plŇćg "land for ploughing, (later) plough". In its latter senses it is a metaphorical extension from the agricultural use, seen from the fifteenth century.
