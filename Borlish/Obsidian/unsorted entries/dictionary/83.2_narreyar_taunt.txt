83.2: narreyar "taunt"

narreyar /ňĆna.riňąjar/ [ňĆna. Ä…™ňąj…Ďňź]
- taunt, mock, make fun of, to say or do something which makes someone seem foolish;
- goad, provoke, to cause someone to respond to you annoyed or angry;
- (intransitive) jest, fool around, to tell jokes or otherwise act in a comedic manner

Etymology: via Old Boral nari»Ěar "to taunt, goad" from Vulgar Latin nńĀricŇć "I snort", related to nńĀrńďs "nose". Respelt in the Modern Boral period by conflation with narr "fool, jester, joker", which was borrowed some centuries earlier from Germanic (cf. Saxon narr "wildcard; maverick").
