167.4: salubr "virtuous"

salubr /saňąl…™brŐ©/ [s…źňąl…™.b…ź]
- healthy, healthful, good for someone's health;
- beneficial, advantageous, expedient, generally good for someone or something;
- virtuous, righteous, honourable, principled, having good moral character;
- generous, magnanimous, charitable, willing to give and share things unsparingly

Etymology: Middle Borlish, borrowed directly from Latin salŇęber "healthy, beneficial". The terms has been applied to moral goodness almost from the moment of its first attestation in Borlish. In the monetary sense the word has been used since the eighteenth century.
