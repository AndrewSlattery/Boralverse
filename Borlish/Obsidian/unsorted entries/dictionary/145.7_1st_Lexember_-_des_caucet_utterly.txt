145.7: 1st Lexember - des caucet "utterly"

des caucet /d…õz koňąts…õt/ [d…õz k äňąts…õt]
- utterly, absolutely, downright, to the fullest extent possible;
- completely, totally, all the way, all-out, every inch, done to completion;
- irreversibly, irretrievably, irrevocably, in a way that cannot be taken back

Etymology: idiom attested from the late nineteenth century, literally "out of (one's) socks". Thought to have arisen in Damvath slang as a sexual metaphor (taking one's socks off prior to sleeping with someone), although in modern usage it is not considered at all obscene.
