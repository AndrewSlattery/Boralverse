164.2: cardonar "unknot"

cardonar /ňĆkar.doňąnar/ [ňĆk…źňź.d äňąn…źňź]
- card, tease, to separate and align the fibres of wool, etc;
- comb, curry, to run a carding tool through fur or hair;
- disentangle, unknot, to unfasten a knotted string or to separate several entwined strings;
- sort through, organise, to go over a collection of things and separate them into groups;
- resolve, sort out, simplify, clarify, streamline, to make a situation less complex

Etymology: verbal derivative from Old Borlish cart "carding tool" (since obsoleted by cardon, a synonymous variant). This is a descendant of Latin carduus or cardŇć "wild thistle". The verb has been used for metaphorical disentangling since at least the fourteenth century.
