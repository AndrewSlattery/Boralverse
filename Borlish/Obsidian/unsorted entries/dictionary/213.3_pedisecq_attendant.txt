213.3: pedisecq "attendant"

pedisecq /ňĆpe.diňąz…õk/ [ňĆpe.d…™ňąz…õk]
- attendant, servant, page, valet, a person employed to see to a person's needs;
- goon, henchperson, thug, an enforcer for an organised crime syndicate;
- lackey, follower, toady, sycophant, a servile flatterer

Etymology: early modern period as a direct borrowing from Latin pedisequus "attendant", used as a general term encompassing the pre-existent specific terms for various types of personal servant. The Latin term literally means "following on foot", from pńďs "foot" and sequor "I follow". The word had gained generally pejorative connotations by the twentieth century, reinforced by its use in media depicting gangs and similar groups.
