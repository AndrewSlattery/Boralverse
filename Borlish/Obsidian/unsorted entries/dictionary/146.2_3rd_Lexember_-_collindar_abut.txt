146.2: 3rd Lexember - collindar "abut"

collindar /ňĆk…Ē.l…™nňądar/ [ňĆk…Ē.l…™nňąd…źňź]
- abut, neighbour, border, to lie adjacent to (as of a piece of territory);
- run up against, bump into, to start encountering problems with;
- resemble, recall, to remind one of due to similarity

Etymology: regular descendant of Latin collimitŇć "I border on", from limitŇć "I delimit, bound". Alternatively, a derivation in the Old Borlish period from an unattested lindar or now-dialectal lint "fence". Extension to conceptual closeness is attested from the seventeenth century.
