215.2: ou sunt ou soluð "take it or leave it"

ou sunt ou soluð /u sɪnt u soˈlɪθ/ [u sɪnt u sʊˈlɪθ]
- take it or leave it, expression asserting that something is fixed regardless of whether the listener accepts it;
- either way, no matter what, regardless, no different under any circumstances

Etymology: idiom attested from the seventeenth century, literally "whether used or discarded". The archaic coordinating construction ou … ou "either … or" (now usually ou … ben) descends directly from Latin aut "or", while the past participles come from poetic sumir "seize, take, use" < Latin sūmō "I get" and souvr "let go, release" < Latin solvō "I loosen, untie".
