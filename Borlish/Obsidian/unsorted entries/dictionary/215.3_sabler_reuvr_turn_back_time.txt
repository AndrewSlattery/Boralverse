215.3: sabler reuvr "turn back time"

sabler reuvr /saˈblɛr rauvr̩/ [sɐˈblɛː ˈʀau.vɐ]
- turn back time, (used literally) to travel into the past;
- try again, have a do-over, get another chance, to be permitted to disregard some result and attempt something anew;
- live in the past, to dwell (usually overmuch) on memories or nostalgia;
- (computing) reset, to restore to a previous state

Etymology: idiom attested from the nineteenth century, literally "to flip (an) hourglass". The noun sabler "hourglass" is a derivative of sabr "sand" influenced by synonymous sablier in French, from Latin sabulō "sand, gravel". The verb reuvr "flip, upend" descends directly from Latin revolvō "I unwind; I turn over". Originally restricted to metaphor, the phrase is attested in literal use from the late nineteenth century in parachthon [sci-fi, fantasy] works of writing.
