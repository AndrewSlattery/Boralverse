23.3: annexar "supplement"

annexar /ňĆa.n…õkňąsar/ [ňĆa.n…™gňąz…Ďňź]
- to supplement, append, augment, to add something extra in order to improve something or make it bigger;
- to boost, bolster, strengthen, to help something to increase or improve;
- to enhance, refine, cultivate, to permit or cause something to grow and develop

Etymology: first used in Borlish in the post-Revitalist era, a borrowing from Scholastic Latin annexŇć "I adjoin, attach". This is a verbal derivation from Classical annexus "tied, fastened, attached", past participle of annectŇć "I bind, connect".
