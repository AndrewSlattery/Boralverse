110.4: n'oc hour "for a while"

n'oc hour /n…Ēk ur/ [n äňąg äňź]
- for a while, for a short time, awhile, from now until a point not far in the future;
- for now, provisionally, temporarily, presently, at the moment but likely not for long;
- (sometimes proscribed) soon, imminently, about to occur

Etymology: sixteenth-century idiom, literally "in this time". The preposition and demonstrative n'oc < Latin in hŇćc "in this" co-occur also in other contemporary idioms like n'oc ort "upwards, with an upwards motion". In the last sense the phrase is still sometimes proscribed, despite attestations going back over two hundred years.

J'asseye n'oc hour fin a tandem alandau my convey.
