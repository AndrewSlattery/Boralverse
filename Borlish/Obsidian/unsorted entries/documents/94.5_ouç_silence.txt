Southbar (Borlish Sothbar /s蓴胃藞bar/ [s蕣h藞b蓱藧]) is a region of Borland in the southwest of the island. Its largest population centre is the city of Axbane.
History

The petty kingdom of Southbar was founded by Anglian immigrants in the fifth century, along with Angland and several more minor kingdoms. It quickly established itself in opposition to Angland and to the rump sub-Roman Borlish state. At the turn of the ninth century it saw an influx of immigration from East Anglia after it was subjugated by Markland.

After an alliance between the First Kingdom of Borland and Angland and the Battle of Fendal (810), Southbar was forced to swear fealty as a subordinate kingdom.

Southbar held out for some time against the Danes after the fall of the First Kingdom of Borland, and even harboured the exiled King Jothey and his wife Brenna of Barrow. Nonetheless, by the end of the tenth century Sothbar had been incorporated into Dane Borland.

In the twelfth century Southbar had become a county. Its de facto seat was Axbane; by 1190 Count Steven is known to have resided year-round in the coastal town, bequeathing the old house in Heitri莽 to his younger brother.
