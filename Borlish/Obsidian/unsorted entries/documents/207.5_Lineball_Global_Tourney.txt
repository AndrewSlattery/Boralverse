207.5: Lineball Global Tourney

collected notes on a sporting event.

The Lineball Global Tourney (Borlish: Torne Mondial a Lançbal /tɔrˈne mɔnˈdjal a lantsˈbal/), often abbreviated in the English languages to the LGT, is a knock-out competition in which teams from around the world compete in the sport of lineball. The LGT has been held every five years since 1920, and it has grown from a dozen entrants to the modern complement of over sixty. The precise line-up of each tourney is affected by the ways geopolitics interacts with the institution's founding charter, as well as economic considerations (which is to say bribery of the bilateral committee).

In principle, teams in the LGT represent nations or at least polities. In practice, the complexity of defining what counts as a polity (an entire alliance-federacy? A province? Half of a double-crown?)—as well as the inequity of polities having wildly different sizes of talent pool—have led to guidelines defining loose bounds on the population size of applicant polities.

The sport of lineball was formalised many decades before the establishment of the LGT, and there were several small-scale international lineball competitions that preceded it. For instance, the Cup of Cappatia [South America] was first held in Awasúconda [~Paraná] in 1908, pitting twenty city teams from various Cappatian staddomains against each other.

The 1935 LGT was the first to be held in Mendeva [North America], hosted by Hasiny [~lower Mississippi watershed]. The territory of Norman Kent, which was then a constituent of the Drengot Collusion, was a competitor whose team included one Vréqueu Margouille. His son, Vincent, also became a professional lineball player, and indeed he captained Kent to its only victory in the 1965 LGT.
